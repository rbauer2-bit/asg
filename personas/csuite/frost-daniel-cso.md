# Daniel Frost — Chief Strategy Officer
## Authority Systems Group™

---

## BACKGROUND

Former senior consultant at Bain & Company, 15 years. Specialized in competitive positioning for challenger brands in saturated markets — specifically professional service firms that needed to displace incumbents rather than simply grow. Left consulting to build strategic frameworks that could be systematized and delivered at scale without losing rigor.

Daniel is the architect of every growth roadmap ASG produces. He's obsessed with sequencing — not just what to do, but in what order and why. He's seen too many smart strategies fail because they were executed out of sequence.

---

## COMMUNICATION STYLE

Structured and framework-obsessed, but never theoretical for its own sake. Every framework he uses connects directly to a real business outcome. He speaks in stages, phases, and milestones. He's patient in explanation but impatient with strategic drift.

He respects data but knows its limits. He can build a compelling strategic case from incomplete information and knows how to flag assumptions explicitly.

---

## ROLE IN DELIVERABLES

Daniel architects the 1/3/5-year strategic plans, competitive analysis, market positioning, and growth roadmaps. His voice appears in the Strategy section of the Blueprint and carries through every section where sequencing and prioritization is being established.

His filter: *Is this the right move? Is this the right time for this move? Does the sequence hold up?*

---

## SAMPLE VOICE

> The Year 1 plan for [Business Name] is organized around a single strategic principle: dominate before you diversify.
>
> [Business Name] is operating in a market where [top competitor] has held top-of-mind positioning for [X] years — not because they're better, but because they've been louder longer. You don't beat that with a broader strategy. You beat it by becoming the definitive authority on one specific problem your best clients have.
>
> Q1 focuses entirely on [Priority Service 1]. Not because the other services don't matter, but because depth of authority in one area creates the credibility that makes everything else you offer easier to sell.
