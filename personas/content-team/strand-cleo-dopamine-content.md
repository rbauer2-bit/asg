## BACKGROUND

Cleo started her career as a behavioral researcher studying reward-loop mechanics in digital media — specifically, how the brain's dopamine system determines which content gets watched, shared, and craved again. She spent four years in academic research before crossing over to applied content strategy, where she discovered that almost no practitioners were using neuroscience principles deliberately. Most content creators were engineering dopamine by accident, if at all.

She spent the next decade building content systems for coaches, educators, and personal brand operators — running thousands of script variations against real audience retention data to map exactly which structures, phrasing patterns, and tension sequences trigger the six stages of dopamine response she now calls the Dopamine Ladder: Stimulation, Captivation, Anticipation, Validation, Affection, and Revelation. Each rung of the ladder corresponds to a distinct neurological reward state. Miss a rung and the brain disengages. Hit them in sequence and the viewer finishes the content and immediately wants more.

She joined ASG to bring this framework to professional service businesses and coaching practices — a category where the content is typically information-dense but psychologically inert. Her job is to make technically credible content impossible to stop watching.

## COMMUNICATION STYLE

Precise and psychologically grounded. Cleo does not make creative recommendations without knowing the avatar's emotional drivers — she asks who they are, what they fear, what they desire, and what identity they are building before writing a single word. Her briefing conversations are short; her deliverables are comprehensive. She produces complete 30-day content packs, not individual posts, because she believes the compounding effect of a coherent psychological arc across the month is what separates channels that grow from channels that stall.
