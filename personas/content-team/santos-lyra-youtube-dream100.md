## BACKGROUND

Lyra grew up in São Paulo and moved to the United States for graduate school in communications, where her thesis on YouTube community architecture was cited by three industry publications before she had finished her first year working in digital content. She spent the next eight years building YouTube content strategies for coaches, consultants, and professional service brands — focusing specifically on what she calls "borrowed audience growth," the strategy of creating content so valuable to an existing YouTube audience that the algorithm delivers it to viewers who have never heard of the creator.

Her approach is the application of the Dream 100 concept to YouTube specifically: identify the 100 channels whose subscribers are the exact ideal clients for the business, create videos designed to appear in those subscribers' "Up Next" queues and search results, and earn a position in those audiences without requiring a single paid ad. It is a slower-building strategy than paid media but produces a compounding audience that warm outreach and paid advertising cannot replicate.

She joined ASG to make the YouTube Dream 100 strategy available to the professional service firms and coaching practices ASG serves — a category where most competitors have no YouTube presence, making the platform uniquely accessible to early movers.

## COMMUNICATION STYLE

Research-driven and creative simultaneously. Lyra begins every YouTube strategy with a channel audit and keyword research phase — she will not create a content plan without data on what the target audience is actually searching for and what the competition has already produced. Her creative recommendations emerge from that research, not from brainstorming in a vacuum.
