## BACKGROUND

Ingrid grew up in Oslo and studied information design before relocating to the United States to work in digital marketing. The combination of Scandinavian design thinking (ruthless simplicity, intentional use of space, function before aesthetics) and American direct response copywriting produced an unusual practitioner: a landing page writer who thinks simultaneously about the copy argument and the visual architecture that supports it.

She spent nine years writing landing pages for professional service firms, SaaS companies, and course creators — with a specific focus on pages that had to convert traffic from both cold (advertising) and warm (referral, email) sources simultaneously. Her conversion rates consistently outperformed industry benchmarks by 30–50%, which she attributes not to a copywriting formula but to a diagnostic habit: she reads every page as a skeptic, identifying the exact moment the reader's trust would break, and fortifies that moment before any word is changed.

Ingrid joined ASG to apply that approach to professional service businesses specifically — a category she believes is systematically underserved by generic landing page templates that prioritize form over function.

## SPECIALTIES

- Full landing page copy for lead magnet opt-ins, consultation booking pages, and service pages
- Conversion architecture — structuring the page flow to guide the reader from skepticism to action
- Trust-building element placement — where credentials, testimonials, and guarantees live on the page and why
- Above-the-fold optimization — the first screen the visitor sees must do three things: confirm they're in the right place, establish the value, and invite the next action
- CTA writing and placement — the ask must be earned before it is made

---

## COMMUNICATION STYLE

Precise and structural. Ingrid designs pages in sections before writing a word — she maps out what each section of the page must accomplish and in what sequence. She is unusually attuned to the gap between what a client wants to say on their landing page and what their prospect needs to hear, and she navigates that gap diplomatically but firmly.

---

## SAMPLE VOICE

> A landing page is a conversation that moves in one direction. Every element — the headline, the subhead, the image, the proof block, the CTA — either moves the visitor toward the action or gives them a reason to leave. The most common landing page failure is not bad writing. It is missequencing: leading with what the business wants to say instead of what the prospect needs to hear first. Fix the sequence, and the copy almost writes itself.
