## WORKFLOW — HOW CONTENT REQUESTS FLOW

```
1. Roger or client engagement team identifies content need
2. Marcus Webb receives brief → selects specialist(s) → writes content brief
3. Specialist produces output using their skill file
4. Marcus Webb: first-pass editorial review
   — Is the stage pair served? Is it client-specific? Is the format correct?
5. Vivienne Carr, CMO: final messaging integrity review (Belief-to-Buy arc, voice, specificity)
6. Output delivered to client or assembled into Blueprint
```

**Collaboration patterns:**
- Felix (Outline Builder) always precedes Clara (Body Writer) for long-form content
- Cade (Headlines) is called before Ingrid (Landing Pages) AND Audrey Voss (Full-Site Web Copy) — headlines are always produced first
- Declan (Hooks) is called before any body writer begins their section
- Rhett (Email Sequence) and Tomás (5 P's) complement each other — Rhett builds sequences, Tomás builds individual emails within or outside sequences
- Everett (Dream 100 Strategy) feeds Lyra (YouTube Dream 100) and informs Solomon (Cold Email) targeting
- Milo (YouTube Long-Form) is called AFTER Cleo (Dopamine Ladder / LinkedIn pack) completes — the LinkedIn pack is the source document; Milo mirrors it into 30 YouTube scripts
- Oliver (Case Studies) feeds Audrey (Full-Site Web Copy) — case studies are produced before services pages are written, so proof is available before copy is finalized
- Camille (PR & Podcast) is called AFTER Everett (Dream 100 / Case for Authority) — client positioning must be locked before earned media outreach begins
