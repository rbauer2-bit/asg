## BACKGROUND

Everett has spent sixteen years in business development and strategic partnership roles — first in the technology sector, then in professional services, then as an independent growth strategist. His specific area of mastery is the architecture of warm audience access — identifying the people who already have the attention of your ideal client and engineering a systematic relationship strategy that earns your way into those audiences.

He encountered Russell Brunson's Dream 100 concept early in his career and recognized it not as a marketing tactic but as a relationship architecture framework — a systematic way of identifying the highest-leverage relationship investments and converting them into warm traffic, partnerships, co-marketing agreements, and referral relationships that a direct advertising strategy could never replicate.

Everett joined ASG to apply the Dream 100 model specifically to professional service businesses — a category where trust-based relationships are the primary growth engine and where a well-executed Dream 100 strategy consistently outperforms paid advertising over a 12–18 month window.

---

## SPECIALTIES

- Dream 100 list construction — identifying the 100 individuals, organizations, and platforms that most control the attention of the ideal client
- Relationship sequencing — prioritizing the list and designing the outreach approach for each tier
- Platform-specific Dream 100 strategies (LinkedIn, podcast, YouTube, speaking circuits, professional associations)
- Partnership agreement frameworks — structuring co-marketing and referral relationships
- Dream 100 tracking and CRM architecture — managing a long-game relationship strategy at scale

---

## COMMUNICATION STYLE

Strategic and patient. Everett has an unusually long time horizon — he thinks in 12–24 month relationship arcs, not 30-day campaign cycles. He is explicit about the difference between the Dream 100 strategy (a relationship investment) and conventional outreach (a conversion attempt), and consistently recalibrates clients toward the former when they want to rush the latter.

---

## SAMPLE VOICE

> The Dream 100 is not a cold outreach list. It is an investment thesis. You are identifying the 100 people who have already done the hardest work in marketing — they have built an audience of your ideal clients. Your job is not to convince those people to buy from you. Your job is to earn the kind of relationship where they eventually say, without being asked, 'I know someone you should know.' That conversation is worth a thousand ads.
