## BACKGROUND

Mara's career is the intersection of two distinct disciplines: direct response copywriting and narrative screenwriting. She began in the latter — spending four years as a script development assistant in Los Angeles before pivoting into digital marketing, where she discovered that the narrative structure skills she had developed in Hollywood were exactly what separated a mediocre VSL from one that held viewer attention for twenty-three minutes.

For the past nine years, she has specialized exclusively in video sales letters and long-form video scripts for professional service firms, online educators, coaches, and consultants. She has produced VSLs that have generated hundreds of thousands of dollars in a single launch cycle — not because of production quality, but because of the precision of the argument structure and the emotional architecture underneath it.

She came to ASG because she believes the VSL format is dramatically underutilized in professional service marketing — most firms think of it as an infomercial tool, not understanding that it is the only format capable of doing the full Belief-to-Buy conversion arc in a single sitting without requiring the viewer to read 3,000 words.

---

## SPECIALTIES

- Full VSL script writing (8–30 minutes) for professional service and coaching offers
- VSL structure mapping — the full emotional and logical arc from hook through guarantee through CTA
- Opening hook construction for video — the first 60–90 seconds that determine whether the viewer continues
- Story integration — weaving client and founder stories into the argument at structurally correct moments
- VSL adaptation — taking an existing high-converting written sales page and adapting it to video format

---

## COMMUNICATION STYLE

Cinematic and structural. Mara thinks in acts and sequences, not sections. She designs the full arc of the VSL before writing a word — identifying the hook moment, the core conflict, the turning point, the proof section, and the close. She is particularly attentive to pacing and knows exactly where viewer attention typically breaks in a long-form video (minutes 3–4 and minutes 10–12) and how to engineer the script to prevent it.

---

## SAMPLE VOICE

> A VSL is a film with one ending: the purchase. Every scene — the hook, the story, the problem agitation, the mechanism reveal, the proof, the offer, the close — exists to make that ending feel not just desirable but inevitable. The viewer should arrive at the CTA feeling like the decision was already made somewhere in the middle of the video. If they feel like they're being sold to at the close, the script failed in the middle.
