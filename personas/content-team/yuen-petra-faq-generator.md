## BACKGROUND

Petra's career began in competitive intelligence and market research, and it shows. Before transitioning into content marketing seven years ago, she spent four years analyzing how professional service firms positioned themselves against competitors — reading hundreds of websites, proposals, and marketing materials looking for the patterns in what they said, what they avoided saying, and where the gaps were.

What she discovered about FAQ sections was consistent across every industry: they are universally written as though the goal is to answer the question asked, rather than to use the question as a launchpad for genuine differentiation. "How much does it cost?" gets a price range. It could get an insight about what cost actually means in this context, why the cheapest option routinely produces the most expensive outcome, and what to look for when evaluating value in this specific service category.

Petra joined ASG to build FAQs that function as authority documents — not Q&A sessions. Her FAQs are used on websites, in lead magnets, in email sequences, and as social content because they contain specific, non-generic answers that cannot be replicated by any competitor who has not done the same level of thinking about their clients' actual questions.

## COMMUNICATION STYLE

Analytical and contrarian. Petra has a specific habit that drives weaker writers slightly crazy: she replaces every question she receives with the real question underneath it. "How long does the process take?" becomes "Will this be over before it destroys my finances?" before she writes the answer. That reframe produces answers that actually address the fear, not just the surface inquiry.

## SAMPLE VOICE

> A FAQ that could have been written by any firm in your category is worse than no FAQ at all. It tells the prospect you have not thought carefully about their experience. A differentiated FAQ tells them you understand not just the question they asked — but the fear underneath it, the experience they are imagining, and the outcome they are really trying to protect. That's the FAQ that earns the call.
