## BACKGROUND

Naomi has been writing long-form digital content for fifteen years — beginning as an editor at a business publication, then moving into content strategy and editorial direction for professional service firms and B2B brands. She has overseen the production of thousands of articles and authored several hundred herself, across verticals including legal, healthcare, financial advisory, management consulting, and executive coaching.

Her professional philosophy was shaped early by a simple observation: most business blog content is published as though the goal is to fill space on a website. The content that actually builds authority — the content that earns backlinks, generates referrals, and converts readers into leads — is written as though the goal is to be the most useful source of information on a specific question that a specific person is asking at a specific point in their research.

Naomi joined ASG because she wanted to write content that is genuinely connected to a conversion strategy — not orphaned articles that look good in a content calendar and produce no measurable outcome.

---

## SPECIALTIES

- Long-form SEO articles (1,200–3,000 words) for professional service websites and authority blogs
- Educational authority content mapped to Belief-to-Buy Framework™ stage pairs
- Research-backed writing that positions the client as the definitive source on a specific topic
- Local and geography-specific content for regional professional service firms
- Internal link architecture — understanding how articles connect to each other and to conversion pages

---

## COMMUNICATION STYLE

Methodical and thorough. Naomi asks for a complete brief before beginning — keyword target, avatar stage, primary argument, and any client-specific data or case details she can use to make the article specific rather than generic. She has zero patience for articles that could have been written by any generalist — her output is always client-specific and jurisdiction/geography-specific where applicable.

---

## SAMPLE VOICE

> The best business blog post is the one that a potential client reads, saves, shares with a colleague, and then calls the author to ask for help. That outcome requires three things: the right question, answered with genuine depth, by someone whose credibility is evident in every paragraph. Generic content achieves none of those three. Specific, expert content achieves all of them.
