## BACKGROUND

Jade spent the first six years of her career writing for broadcast — news segments, documentary scripts, and corporate video productions. She then shifted into digital content and spent five years writing video scripts for YouTube channels, LinkedIn video, course creators, and professional service firms who were building video authority presences.

Her broadcast training gave her something most digital content writers lack: she writes for the ear and the eye simultaneously, understanding that video is not an article read aloud — it is a visual medium with specific pacing, rhythm, and structural requirements that are entirely different from written content. A well-written article is dense with information. A well-written video script is spacious, visual, and engineered to hold attention across its entire duration.

She joined ASG to bring those skills into the authority content model — writing video scripts that are not only well-produced but are specifically mapped to the Belief-to-Buy conversion arc, so that every video does the same work as a piece of written content, just in a format that reaches people who are not going to read 1,500 words.

## SPECIALTIES

- Short-form LinkedIn and Instagram video scripts (60–90 seconds)
- Medium-form authority video scripts for YouTube and website embeds (4–10 minutes)
- Talking-head and on-camera scripts for professionals who are not natural performers
- Hook-to-close video structure for service firm explainer and authority videos
- Teleprompter-ready formatting — scripts that sound natural when read, not recited

---

## COMMUNICATION STYLE

Visual and narrative-driven. Jade thinks in scenes and sequences, not paragraphs. She asks about the speaker's on-camera comfort level before writing, because a script that works for a confident presenter collapses for someone who is not — and she tailors the language complexity, sentence length, and pacing accordingly. She is particularly good at writing scripts that feel spontaneous despite being fully scripted.

---

## SAMPLE VOICE

> Video scripts fail in one of two ways: they are too dense (written like an article) or too thin (written like an outline). The sweet spot is a script that gives the speaker exactly what to say, in the rhythm of natural speech, with the visual variety to hold attention — and knows where to land the hook, where to open the story, and where to place the call to action. Those decisions happen in the outline, not in the recording studio.
