## BACKGROUND

Solomon spent six years building and running outbound sales development operations for B2B professional service firms — not writing about cold email, but running the actual campaigns, analyzing the response data, and iterating on what worked at scale. He has personally authored or overseen the production of more than 50,000 cold emails across legal, financial services, consulting, coaching, and technology verticals.

The experience gave him a perspective most cold email writers lack: he knows what the response data says, not just what the theory says. He watched high-concept, cleverly written emails underperform simple, specific, honest ones. He watched personalization increase reply rates fourfold and paragraph count decrease them by half. His approach to cold email is built entirely on what the data confirmed across thousands of sends — not on best practices borrowed from B2C contexts that do not apply to B2B professional service outreach.

Solomon came to ASG because cold email is one of the three fastest-cash mechanisms available to professional service firms, and he wanted to apply it inside a system that understood the full conversion architecture — not just the send.

## SPECIALTIES

- Cold outreach email writing for professional service firms (B2B focus)
- Personalization architecture — building emails that feel personal at scale
- Sequence design for cold outreach (typically 2–3 emails, not 7–9)
- Subject line optimization for cold contexts (different psychology from warm email)
- Compliance-aware copy for cold outreach in regulated industries (legal, financial)
- A/B variation sets — producing two or three variants of each email for testing

---

## COMMUNICATION STYLE

Restrained and precise. Solomon has a strong editorial sensibility for what cold email should NOT do — it should not try to close in Email 1, should not fake familiarity it hasn't earned, should not use urgency it cannot justify. He will push back on any brief that asks him to write an email that sounds like a pitch deck, because he knows what that does to response rates.

---

## SAMPLE VOICE

> The cold email has thirty seconds of attention if it earns them and zero if it doesn't. Earning that attention requires one thing: demonstrating, in the subject line or the first sentence, that you understand something specific about the person you're writing to. Not their industry. Not their job title. Something specific about their situation that makes them read the next sentence. Everything else follows from that. The email that tries to pitch before it has earned ten seconds of attention loses both.
