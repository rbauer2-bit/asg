## AUDIT SCORING MODEL

The full marketing audit scores across 6 dimensions:

| Category | Weight | Lead Specialist |

| Content & Messaging | 25% | Lena Park |
| Conversion Optimization | 20% | Marcus Cole |

| Growth & Strategy | 10% | Clay Donovan |

**Overall Marketing Score** = Weighted average (0–100)

| Score | Grade | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| 85–100 | A | Excellent — minor optimizations only |
| 70–84 | B | Good — clear opportunities for improvement |
| 55–69 | C | Average — significant gaps to address |
| 40–54 | D | Below average — major overhaul needed |
| 0–39 | F | Critical — fundamental marketing issues |

## WORKFLOW — HOW AUDIT REQUESTS FLOW

```
1. Roger or client engagement team identifies audit need
2. Victor Hale receives brief → confirms URL and business type
3. All 5 specialists launch in parallel (subagent execution)
4. Victor aggregates findings → applies composite scoring model
5. Victor produces MARKETING-AUDIT.md with full report
6. Cross-team integration:
   — Messaging findings → Vivienne Carr (CMO) for content strategy
   — Competitive findings → Daniel Frost (CSO) for market analysis
   — Technical findings → Iris Nolan (CTO) for digital infrastructure
   — Conversion findings → Tanya Blackwood (CRO) for Fast Cash Sprint™
```

**Integration with Authority Blueprint™:**
- Audit findings can replace or supplement Section 3 (Market Analysis)
- Competitive intelligence feeds Section 5 (1-Year Strategic Plan)
- Conversion and messaging gaps inform Section 8 (Content Authority Strategy™)
- Technical findings connect to Section 9 (Niche Website Strategy)
