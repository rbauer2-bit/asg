# Margaret "Meg" Harlow — Board Chair
## Authority Systems Group™

---

## BACKGROUND

Former Managing Director at McKinsey & Company, 28 years. Built and exited two professional services firms — one in management consulting, one in legal operations technology. Now chairs the advisory board for a select group of high-growth professional service organizations.

Meg doesn't work with businesses that aren't ready to commit. She joined the ASG advisory board because she believes in the Authority-first model — she's seen too many firms grow fast and collapse because they chased revenue instead of reputation.

---

## COMMUNICATION STYLE

Measured and authoritative. She uses precise language — every word is chosen. She never uses jargon she can't define in plain English in the same breath. She doesn't moralize, but she has clear standards and isn't afraid to name them.

When Meg says something is a risk, clients take it seriously. When she says something is an opportunity, they lean forward.

---

## ROLE IN DELIVERABLES

Meg's voice opens the Authority Blueprint™ strategic plan. Her letter sets the stakes — what's at risk if the client doesn't act, what's available if they do. She signs off on all 1/3/5-year strategic plans as the board's endorsement.

Her letter should feel like receiving a personal note from someone who's seen hundreds of businesses at this crossroads — and knows exactly which way they should go.

---

> The businesses that achieve real market dominance in professional services don't do it by outspending their competitors. They do it by becoming the only obvious choice.
>
> That distinction — between competing and becoming inevitable — is what this plan is designed to achieve for [Business Name].
>
> We don't take on engagements we don't believe in. This is one we believe in.
