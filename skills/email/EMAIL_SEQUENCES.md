## BLUEPRINT DELIVERY STANDARD — READ BEFORE WRITING ANY SEQUENCE

Every email sequence included in an Authority Blueprint™ must be **fully written**. This means:

- **Complete subject line** — written to the avatar's current belief/emotion stage, not to the product
- **Full body copy** — every paragraph, every sentence, opening to sign-off
- **Compliance footer** — firm identification + opt-out on every email
- **No structural placeholders** — "insert email copy here," "[subject line]," or "draft to come" are failures

The Blueprint is a deployment-ready document. A client should be able to hand Section 10 to an assistant and have every sequence live within one week. That is the standard.

**Minimum sequences in every Blueprint (Section 10):**

| # | Sequence | Emails | Duration |
|---|----------|--------|----------|
| 1 | Past Client Reactivation | 5 | 18 days |
| 2 | Referral Capture — from happy clients | 3 | 12 days |
| 3 | Welcome Sequence — new leads | 5 | 7 days |
| 4 | Professional Referral Partner Outreach | 3 | 21 days |
| 5 | Identity Self-Selection Sequence | 5 | pre-consultation stage |

**The Identity Self-Selection Sequence is the highest-leverage sequence in the system.** If a client deploys nothing else, this sequence alone will pre-qualify pipeline, eliminate misaligned prospects, and create an inevitability effect for aligned readers. It does not sell. It does not pitch. It sorts. Right prospects feel seen and steady. Wrong prospects quietly disengage. The next step becomes inevitable for anyone who belongs there. Required for every engagement without exception.

Additional sequences (post-appointment follow-up, upsell/cross-sell, flash offer, loyalty touchpoint) are included when relevant to the client's fast cash priorities.

## MASTER EMAIL PRINCIPLE

Every email in every sequence has ONE job: move the prospect one step forward on one or both tracks of the Belief-to-Buy™ framework.

Email is not a broadcast channel. It is a one-to-one belief and emotion movement system operating at scale.

**The ASG Email Standards**:
- Every email is written to one person (even when sent to thousands)
- Every email has a named stage pair target before a single word is written
- Every email has exactly one primary call-to-action
- No email opens with "I" as the first word
- No email uses "just checking in" — every email earns its place in the sequence with a specific job
- Every email subject line is written to the avatar's current belief or emotional state — not to the product being offered

**Compliance note for all professional service sequences**: All sequences must pass through `niche-library/[niche-id].yaml compliance_flags` before finalization. Medical and legal niches in particular have specific restrictions on outcome claims, urgency framing, and testimonial use.

## PURPOSE

The Welcome Sequence is the most important sequence in the system. A new lead is at maximum receptivity — they just opted in. The first 7 days determine whether they file [Client Name] as "another provider" or elevate them to "the authority I trust on this topic."

Most welcome sequences waste this window with "thanks for subscribing" emails. The ASG Welcome Sequence uses it to do 5 weeks of belief-building work in 7 days.

## THE 5-EMAIL STRUCTURE

### EMAIL 1 — THE PATTERN INTERRUPT WELCOME
**Day**: Send immediately
**Stage pair**: B1/E1 — Surface dissatisfaction, signal the Enemy Belief exists
**Subject line strategy**: Speaks to their current state, not the service. Creates recognition, not promotion.
**Tone**: Personal. Roger Bauer's voice. Sounds like a note from a specific person, not an automated system.

**Structure**:
- Opening: Name what just brought them here (the trigger event or search that led to the opt-in)
- Middle: One insight — something that makes them feel immediately understood. NOT a product pitch.
- Close: A teaser of what's coming in the sequence — make them want the next email
- CTA: Read the linked content (anchor piece or video) — soft engagement

**The one job**: Make the prospect feel like they landed in the right place. Produce recognition, not interest.

**Subject line examples** (to be written specifically for this client/niche):
- "[Niche-specific trigger situation]? Read this before you do anything else."
- "Most [avatar type] get this backwards — and it costs them."
- "Before we go any further — something I need you to know."

---

### EMAIL 2 — THE ENEMY BELIEF EMAIL
**Day**: Day 2
**Stage pair**: B1/E1 → B2/E2 — Name and dismantle the Enemy Belief, introduce contrast
**Tone**: Empathetic truth-teller. Validates why they formed the belief before challenging it.

**Structure**:
- Opening: "There's a belief most [avatar type] have before they [relevant action] — and it's costing them."
- Name the Enemy Belief explicitly — in their own language, from their own perspective
- Validate it: "Here's why that makes complete sense — and why it formed."
- The reframe: "Here's why it's wrong — and what's actually true."
- Contrast: Brief before/after — what changes when they operate on the correct belief
- CTA: A deeper content piece that builds on the reframe (video or article)

**The one job**: Remove the Enemy Belief. Plant the Pre-Frame. This email does more conversion work than any other in the sequence.

---

### EMAIL 3 — THE NEW WORLDVIEW EMAIL
**Day**: Day 3-4
**Stage pair**: B2/E2 → B3/E3 — Build the coherent new explanation, activate desire
**Tone**: Confident teacher. The guide, not the salesperson.

**Structure**:
- Opens with a bridge from Email 2: "Yesterday I told you [enemy belief reframe]. Today I want to show you what's actually true."
- Deliver the New Worldview in 3-4 clear points — the coherent explanation that replaces fragments
- The before/after contrast made specific: what does the avatar's situation look like under the new worldview?
- Seed desire: paint the after-state briefly but specifically
- CTA: The long-form anchor piece or video for this service (the deep dive)

**The one job**: Build the New Worldview completely enough that the avatar can explain their situation differently after reading it.

---

### EMAIL 4 — THE INTERNAL ALIGNMENT EMAIL
**Day**: Day 5
**Stage pair**: B3/E3 → B4/E4 — Explain past failures, produce urgency
**Tone**: Story-driven, specific, emotionally grounded

**Structure**:
- Opens with a case or example: a client who was in the exact situation the avatar is in
- How past attempts failed — and why (framed through the New Worldview lens)
- The "I finally understood" moment — when Internal Alignment happened for that person
- The cost of waiting: what was lost between when they first had the problem and when they acted
- CTA: A specific case study or outcome story

**The one job**: Make past failures make sense within the new framework. Surface urgency through the cost of the delay the case study character experienced.

---

### EMAIL 5 — THE CONVERGENCE EMAIL
**Day**: Day 7
**Stage pair**: B4/E4 → B5/E5 — Certainty + Relief + Consultation invite
**Tone**: Direct, warm, confident. Roger Bauer's voice — personal and clear.

**Structure**:
- Short recap: "Over the past week I've shown you [3 things — Enemy Belief dismantled, New Worldview, Internal Alignment]"
- The Certainty statement: "If your situation looks like [description], here's what I know about it."
- The Relief statement: Name the next step, describe exactly what happens, remove friction
- The invitation: "If this resonates — here's how to [specific next action]"
- CTA: Consultation booking link or next-step offer

**The one job**: Produce simultaneous B5 Certainty and E5 Relief. This email should not feel like a sales pitch — it should feel like the natural conclusion of a conversation that's been building for a week.

---

## SUBJECT LINE STRATEGY (applies to all 5)

Subject lines must be written to the avatar's CURRENT stage — not to the desired outcome.

- Email 1 (B1/E1): Speaks to their current state or trigger event
- Email 2 (B1→B2): Creates recognition of the Enemy Belief
- Email 3 (B3/E3): Promises the new understanding they're now open to
- Email 4 (B4/E4): Story-based — curiosity around someone's outcome
- Email 5 (B5/E5): The direct invitation — clear, not clever

Subject lines that never work in any of these sequences:
- "Re: Your inquiry" (implies automation)
- "Just wanted to follow up..." (no value offered)
- "Don't miss this!" (fake urgency)
- Any subject that reads like a newsletter

# EMAIL SEQUENCE 4: REFERRAL SEQUENCE (Hidden Revenue Strategy #5)
# Trigger: Case / project completed successfully + positive feedback received

## PURPOSE

The best time to ask for a referral is immediately after a successful outcome — when the client is at peak E5 (Relief) from their own experience and their satisfaction is highest. This sequence captures that window systematically rather than relying on the provider remembering to ask.

---

## THE 3-EMAIL STRUCTURE

### EMAIL 1 — THE OUTCOME CELEBRATION
**Timing**: 2-3 days after successful case close / project completion
**Stage**: E5 — Reinforces their Relief and satisfaction
Opens with: Acknowledgment of the outcome — specific to their situation
Angle: "I wanted to take a moment to recognize what you accomplished."
Content: What was achieved. The contrast from where they started.
Referral seed (NOT ask yet): "The reason I'm reaching out is that I know how rare it is to have a [positive outcome] — and I know not everyone in [similar situation] gets that result."
CTA: None — this email gives, it doesn't take

### EMAIL 2 — THE REFERRAL EDUCATION
**Day 5 | Stage: E3/E4 — Desire for their referral to get the same outcome, Urgency around someone's current situation**
Opens with: "I want to ask you something directly — but first, let me explain why."
Angle: The people who don't get good outcomes are often the ones who didn't know the right questions to ask, or waited too long. Your referral can change that for someone.
The ask: Structured, specific, not generic ("if you know anyone who...") — "If you know someone who is [specific trigger situation], would you be comfortable introducing us? Here's exactly what I'd do for them."
Include: What referring looks like in practice (forward this email, share this link, introduce us over text)
CTA: The referral program details or the simple "reply with their name and I'll take it from there"

### EMAIL 3 — THE EASY ASK FOLLOW-UP
**Day 12 | Stage: E4 — Urgency (for the referral's situation)**
Short. Direct. Personal.
"I know I mentioned this last week — I just want to make it as easy as possible for you."
One-sentence reminder of what the referral gets.
Pre-written text they can copy and send to introduce someone.
CTA: The simplest possible action — "If you know someone, forward this to them with a note. That's it."

# EMAIL SEQUENCE 9: IDENTITY SELF-SELECTION SEQUENCE
# Trigger: New lead before first consultation — any pre-call, pre-sale, pre-consult stage
# Stage flow: Attention → Relevance → Interpretation → Possibility → Action (Identity Commitment)
# Goal: Force internal self-classification between two polarized avatars; no selling required
# Length: 5 emails — spacing at practitioner's discretion per niche

---

## PURPOSE

This sequence does not sell. It does not persuade. It sorts.

By the end of 5 emails, right prospects feel seen and steady. Wrong prospects quietly disengage. The next step becomes inevitable for anyone who belongs there — no CTA required to produce it.

The mechanism is identity self-classification. Prospects assign themselves to one of two polarized behavioral avatars — not because they are told to, but because the description fits so precisely that they cannot avoid the recognition. Aligned readers feel calm certainty. Misaligned readers feel inconvenience, not shame — they simply realize this is not their context.

This is the highest-leverage pre-qualification tool in the ASG system. It removes the need for objection handling because the wrong prospects self-eliminate before they ever reach a consultation.

- validate emotionally

- explain consequences, not opinions
