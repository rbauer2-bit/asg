## SECTION A — THE VIDEO AUTHORITY ARCHITECTURE

### The 4-Series Framework

The year's video content is organized into four interconnected series — one per quarterly service focus. Each series has a distinct purpose within the Belief-to-Buy™ framework:

**Series 1 (Q1 Service): The Enemy Belief Series**
Primary dual-track target: B1/E1 → B2/E2
Purpose: Establishes the authority position by directly addressing the most damaging wrong assumption in the market. This is the series that makes prospects stop and say "how did they know I was thinking that?"
Tone: Empathetic truth-teller. Not salesy. Sounds like a trusted advisor, not a marketer.
Opening video title strategy: Lead with the Enemy Belief as the hook — name the assumption, validate it, then dismantle it.

**Series 2 (Q2 Service): The New Worldview Series**
Primary dual-track target: B2/E2 → B3/E3
Purpose: Builds a coherent framework for understanding the problem that no competitor provides. Each video adds a piece to the new worldview.
Tone: Confident teacher. Evidence-based. Makes complex things simple without dumbing them down.

**Series 3 (Q3 Service): The Proof Series**
Primary dual-track target: B3/E3 → B4/E4
Purpose: Makes the Internal Alignment and Urgency stages concrete through cases, outcomes, and real cost-of-inaction narratives.
Tone: Story-driven. Specific. Outcome-focused. Emotionally grounded.

**Series 4 (Q4 Service): The Certainty + Relief Series**
Primary dual-track target: B4/E4 → B5/E5
Purpose: Removes the last friction. Makes the decision feel safe and obvious. Produces the final stage pair before the buy.
Tone: Transparent, process-forward, generous with information. Zero sales pressure.

Stage pair target: Short-form clips should be clipped from the most emotionally resonant moments — they serve B1/E1 and B2/E2 by interrupting the scroll and pulling prospects into the long-form content

Produced from client case studies
Target: Website, social, email sequences
Frequency: 1 per quarter
Stage pair target: B3-B4 / E3-E4 — proof mobilizers

## SECTION C — THE FOUR FOUNDATION SCRIPTS

Full scripts for the first four long-form videos — one per priority service.

### SCRIPT FORMAT STANDARD

Every script follows the same architecture — a 7-part structure designed to move the prospect through a minimum of 2-3 stage pairs in a single sitting:

```
PART 1 — THE HOOK (0:00-0:45)
Purpose: Stop the skip. Make them feel immediately understood.
Technique: Open in their current state. Not with your credentials.
Never opens with: "Hi I'm [name] and today I'm going to talk about..."
Always opens with: The prospect's reality — their E1 dissatisfaction or B1 Enemy Belief stated plainly.

PART 2 — THE CREDIBILITY BRIDGE (0:45-2:00)
Purpose: Earn the right to teach.
Technique: 60-90 seconds maximum. Relevant experience, specific. Not a bio read.
The formula: "I've [relevant experience] and what I've learned is [key insight that validates why this video matters]."

PART 3 — THE ENEMY BELIEF / PRE-FRAME (2:00-5:00)
Purpose: B1/B2 — Name the assumption, validate why they formed it, start the reframe.
Technique: "Most [avatar type] believe [enemy belief] — and here's why that makes complete sense.
           The problem is, that assumption is costing you [specific thing]."
Note: This part does NOT argue with the belief. It validates the belief's origins, then gently dismantles it.

PART 4 — THE NEW WORLDVIEW / CONTRAST (5:00-12:00)
Purpose: B3/E2-E3 — Build the coherent new explanation, make the before/after visible.
Technique: Teaching section. Uses examples, analogies, specifics.
For proof-series videos: this is where the case or story goes.
For educator-series videos: this is where the framework or system is explained.

PART 5 — THE INTERNAL ALIGNMENT (12:00-17:00)
Purpose: B4 — Explain past failures. This makes the new worldview stick.
Technique: "Here's why what you tried before didn't work — and why that wasn't your fault."
The avatar's past attempts are validated, then reframed as evidence FOR the new worldview, not against them.

PART 6 — THE URGENCY SURFACE (17:00-20:00)
Purpose: E4 — Make the cost of delay real and specific. Never manufactured.
Technique: "Every [time unit] that [delay continues], [specific cost]. This isn't pressure — it's math."
Use real data or niche-specific patterns from the niche library.
Compliance check: For legal/medical niches, urgency must be factual not emotional pressure.

PART 7 — THE CLOSE (20:00-end)
Purpose: B5/E5 → CTA — Produce Certainty and Relief, then make the next step obvious.
Technique: Name the decision clearly. De-risk it. Make the action frictionless.
"If what I've described sounds like your situation, here's the next step — and here's exactly what happens when you take it."
CTA must match stage: Long-form video CTAs are typically a deeper content offer (guide, email series) or a low-friction consultation offer — not a "buy now."
```

## SECTION D — THE REPURPOSING SYSTEM

Every long-form video generates 7 additional content assets with no additional filming:

1. **3-5 short-form clips** — most emotionally resonant moments, 60-90 seconds each
2. **1 blog post** — the video script reformatted as a long-form article (SEO value)
3. **1 email** — the core insight from the video delivered to the list with video link
4. **2-3 social posts** — hook lines from the video presented as standalone text posts
5. **1 Google Business post** — the video's core question framed as a local resource

Each long-form video = 13-15 total content assets distributed across the quarter.
