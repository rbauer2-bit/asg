### SECTION B — THE FOUR CONTENT PILLARS

Four pillars frame the entire content strategy — one for each direction prospects need moved:

**PILLAR 1 — ENEMY BELIEF DISMANTLER** (B1/E1 → B2/E2)
Content that surfaces the specific wrong assumption and reframes it. This is the hardest content to write and the most valuable. It opens conversations that would never start otherwise.
- Tone: Empathetic first ("here's why you think that and why it made sense"), challenging second ("here's why that assumption is costing you")
- Formats: Video, long-form article, direct mail, social hook posts
- Volume: 25% of total content — these are the top-of-funnel workhorses

**PILLAR 2 — AUTHORITY EDUCATOR** (B2/E2 → B3/E3)
Content that builds the New Worldview by teaching what prospects don't know and making the Before/After contrast undeniable. This is where most "content marketing" lives — but it only works after Pillar 1 has done its job.
- Tone: Confident, teaching from experience, specific examples
- Formats: Long-form articles, video series episodes, email sequences, guides
- Volume: 35% of total content — the content engine's core

**PILLAR 3 — PROOF MOBILIZER** (B3/E3 → B4/E4)
Content that produces Internal Alignment (past failures explained) and Desire/Urgency (real cost of delay surfaced). Case studies, outcome stories, and data that make the stakes concrete.
- Tone: Specific and story-driven. Numbers where available. Outcomes in the client's language.
- Formats: Case studies, testimonial features, before/after narratives, outcome posts
- Volume: 25% of total content — the conversion engine

**PILLAR 4 — TRUST CLOSER** (B4/E4 → B5/E5)
Content that produces Certainty and Relief simultaneously — the final stage pair before the buy. Addresses the last objection, makes the process feel known and safe, and makes the next step feel obvious.
- Tone: Transparent, process-oriented, de-risking. Makes the consultation feel like the natural conclusion.
- Formats: FAQ content, "what to expect" content, consultation process explainers, guarantee language
- Volume: 15% of total content — bottom-of-funnel, high-conversion

### SECTION E — THE CONTENT COMPOUNDING MODEL

Explain how the content strategy compounds across the four quarters:

**Q1 Foundation**: Content for Service #1 establishes the authority position. The Enemy Belief dismantler content begins working on the broadest audience first.

**Q2 Expansion**: Service #1 content is still running (SEO indexing, email nurture). Service #2 content launches on top of an established authority base — prospects encounter Service #2 content already trusting [Client Name] from Service #1 exposure.

**Q3 Depth**: By Q3, a prospect who has encountered the content library across Services #1 and #2 has moved significantly along the Belief Track. Service #3 content can enter at a higher stage pair because the earlier content has already done the Enemy Belief and Pre-Frame work.

**Q4 Authority Lock**: By Q4, the full content library represents 40+ pieces across all four services. A prospect doing research finds [Client Name] everywhere — across all the topics that matter to them. That breadth is itself a Trust Closer (B5/E5). The authority position is no longer claimed — it's demonstrated.

### SECTION F — BRAND VOICE APPLICATION

Map the client's brand voice (from `client-context.yaml brand_voice`) to specific content tone guidelines:

**Enemy Belief Dismantler content**:
- Opens by naming the belief without judgment — validates where it came from
- Shifts to reframe with authority, not arrogance
- Uses "here's what most [niche clients] don't realize..." constructions

**Authority Educator content**:
- Teaching mode — positions [Client Name] as the guide, not the sales person
- Uses specific numbers, case references (anonymized if needed), and outcome language
- Ends with the New Worldview clearly stated — leaves no ambiguity

**Proof Mobilizer content**:
- Story-first — leads with the client's situation at the start (E1 state)
- Moves through the contrast (E2) and into specific outcomes (E3/E4)
- Includes the emotional resolution — how the client FELT after, not just what happened

**Trust Closer content**:
- Maximum transparency — process, timeline, what the client controls
- Addresses the final objection by name — not around it
- CTA is the next logical step, not a sales ask — feels like a continuation, not a close
