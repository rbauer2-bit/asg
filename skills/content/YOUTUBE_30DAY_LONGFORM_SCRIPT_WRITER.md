#### TASK 2: 30 Complete Long-Form Scripts

For each of the 30 days, produce a complete, production-ready script following this structure:

---

**SCRIPT HEADER**
```
VIDEO [#] — [VIDEO TITLE]
Mirror Post: Day [#] LinkedIn Post Title
Stage: [B/E stage pair]
Pillar: [Insider View | Belief Disruptor | Practical Tool | Outcome Story]
Target Length: [X–Y min]
Dopamine Driver: [Primary Level 1–6 hook]
Thumbnail Angle: [1-sentence direction for thumbnail creative]
```

---

**SECTION 1 — HOOK (0:00–0:45)**
Goal: Stop the scroll, trigger a curiosity loop, make leaving feel like a loss.

Produce:
- **Primary Hook** — 1–3 sentences spoken directly to camera. Must match Dopamine Ladder Level 1 (Stimulation). The first sentence is the YouTube equivalent of the LinkedIn post's opening line.
- **Alt Hook A** — alternate opening optimized for returning subscribers (assumes some familiarity)
- **Alt Hook B** — pattern-interrupt opening (provocative, counterintuitive, or data-led)

Include `[B-ROLL: description]` cues for any visuals that should run under the spoken hook.

Then: **The Promise** — 1–2 sentences previewing what the viewer will walk away with. Dopamine Ladder Level 2 (Captivation). This is the "here's why you need to stay" line.

---

**SECTION 2 — SETUP / PROBLEM FRAME (0:45–2:00)**
Goal: Establish stakes. Name the enemy belief. Make the viewer feel seen and frustrated before offering relief.

Dopamine Ladder Level 3 (Anticipation): delay the answer, deepen the problem.

- Spoken script: 3–5 paragraphs naming the problem in the avatar's language
- At least 2 `[B-ROLL: description]` cues (relevant visuals, supporting data, illustrative cutaways)
- End this section without resolving the tension — leave the viewer in productive frustration

---

**SECTION 3 — THE TURN (2:00–3:30)**
Goal: Introduce the reframe. Dopamine Ladder Level 4 (Validation) — the aha moment that rewards the viewer for staying.

- The new worldview or insight that breaks the enemy belief
- Stated with precision, not hedging — client speaks with conviction
- 1–2 `[B-ROLL: description]` cues (framework visuals, examples, supporting evidence)
- This section should feel like a light turned on — not a slow build, a moment of clarity

---

**SECTION 4 — THE PROOF / MECHANISM (3:30–7:00)**
Goal: Show the system, framework, or process that makes the new worldview actionable. Dopamine Ladder Level 5 (Affection) — trust through demonstration.

Structure options (choose the one that fits the topic):
- **Framework walkthrough** — named steps, each explained with specifics
- **Case example** — composite or illustrative story (no real client names without permission)
- **Contrast comparison** — the wrong approach vs. the right approach, side by side

Requirements:
- Minimum 4–6 `[B-ROLL: description]` cues spread throughout
- At least one CHAPTER MARKER suggestion: `[CHAPTER: chapter title at ~X:XX]`
- Content is niche-specific throughout — no line that could belong to a different industry
- Pacing note every 90 seconds: `[PACING: maintain energy / slow down / increase urgency]`

---

**SECTION 5 — THE CLOSE (7:00–END)**
Goal: Dopamine Ladder Level 6 (Revelation) — condition the viewer to trust, subscribe, and act.

Sub-sections:

**5A — Identity Anchor** (~45 seconds)
The viewer should feel that the information they just received is part of who they're becoming, not just something they learned. One paragraph that names the identity shift directly.

**5B — CTA** (~30 seconds)
Stage-appropriate call to action — matches the Belief-to-Buy stage of this video. B1/E1 videos do not ask for a consultation. B4/E4 and B5/E5 videos can direct to the survey or booking link.

CTA pacing:
- Days 1–8: Subscribe / comment / share — no ask beyond engagement
- Days 9–15: Download, resource, or low-commitment next step
- Days 16–22: Mention survey or next step as a possibility, not a pressure
- Days 23–30: Direct CTA to the survey or consultation endpoint

**5C — Channel Hook** (~15 seconds)
The closing line that conditions the viewer to binge the next video. Must be Dopamine Ladder Level 6 caliber — brand-conditioning, identity-level, creates anticipation for the next video in the series.

### QUALITY CONTROL RULES

Every script must satisfy all twelve rules before output is finalized:

1. Every script opens with a hook that would stop a scroll — not a greeting, not a "today we're talking about," not a thank-you for watching
2. The promise (Section 1) is specific — not "I'll share some tips" but "you'll know exactly why your [specific problem] keeps happening and the one shift that stops it"
3. The problem frame (Section 2) must name the avatar's specific frustration in language they would use — not consultant-speak
4. The turn (Section 3) delivers a genuine aha — not a restatement of the problem
5. The proof section (Section 4) contains no generic advice — every claim is niche-specific
6. B-roll cues appear throughout, not clustered — minimum one cue per minute of video
7. Chapter markers are placed at logical payoff points, not arbitrarily
8. CTA stage-matches the Belief-to-Buy pair — no early asks
9. The channel hook (Section 5C) conditions the viewer toward the next video, not just toward a vague sense of enjoying the content
10. Value Bomb and Contrarian variants are genuinely different — not the same script with minor edits
11. Production notes are video-specific — not generic production advice
12. Every line that could apply to a different niche or a different avatar gets rewritten
