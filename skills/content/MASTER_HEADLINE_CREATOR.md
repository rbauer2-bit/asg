### MASTER HEADLINE GENERATION PROMPT (Clarity-First Framework)

#### ROLE & GOAL
You are an expert headline strategist trained in clarity-driven writing. Your task is to generate 10 irresistible, high-clarity headlines that compel the right audience to click by clearly communicating value, relevance, and outcome.
Cleverness is NOT the goal. Clarity, specificity, and relevance are the goal.

---

#### INPUTS (FILL THESE IN BEFORE RUNNING)
- **Niche / Industry:** [Insert niche]
- **Target Audience (Be Specific):** [Who this is for — role, situation, experience level]
- **Primary Topic of the Content:** [What the article/email/video/newsletter is about]
- **Core Problem or Desire:** [What the reader is struggling with or wants]
- **Primary Outcome or Promise:** [What changes for the reader after consuming the content]
- **Tone (optional):** [e.g., analytical, practical, authoritative, conversational]

---

#### HEADLINE RULES (NON-NEGOTIABLE)
Each headline MUST:
1. Be crystal clear at first glance
2. Answer the reader's core questions immediately:
   - What is this about?
   - Is this for me?
   - Why should I care?
3. Use specifics, not vague language
4. Avoid clever wordplay, puns, hype, or shock tactics
5. Create a curiosity gap without giving away the full answer

---

#### HEADLINE COMPONENTS TO USE
Each headline should intentionally incorporate as many of the following as possible:
- **How Many** (number, scope, expectation)
- **What** (tips, reasons, framework, guide, mistakes, etc.)
- **Who** (explicitly or implicitly)
- **Feel** (the reader's emotional state or desired state)
- **Outcome** (the promised result or transformation)

Not every headline must include all five — but every headline must feel complete and specific.

---

#### OUTPUT INSTRUCTIONS
Produce 10 distinct headlines, ensuring:
- No repetition of phrasing or structure
- A mix of audience size (some broader, some more niche)
- A mix of emotional entry points (frustration, clarity, relief, confidence, insight)
- Clear differentiation between each headline

Do NOT explain the headlines. Do NOT add commentary. Only output the numbered list of headlines.

### CLARITY-FIRST HEADLINE TEMPLATE LIBRARY

#### 1. Problem Awareness Templates
*Use when the reader feels pain but hasn't fully articulated it yet.*
1. Why [TARGET AUDIENCE] Struggles With [PROBLEM] (And What Actually Fixes It)
2. The Real Reason [TARGET AUDIENCE] Keeps [NEGATIVE OUTCOME]
3. If You're [TARGET AUDIENCE] and [PROBLEM], This Is Why
4. Most [TARGET AUDIENCE] Don't Realize This Is Causing Their [PROBLEM]
5. The Hidden Cost of [COMMON BEHAVIOR] for [TARGET AUDIENCE]

#### 2. Solution Awareness Templates
*Use when the reader knows the problem and wants direction.*
6. How to [ACHIEVE DESIRED OUTCOME] Without [COMMON OBJECTION]
7. A Simple Way for [TARGET AUDIENCE] to [DESIRED RESULT]
8. The Practical Framework for Fixing [PROBLEM] in [TIMEFRAME or CONTEXT]
9. What Actually Works When [TARGET AUDIENCE] Tries to [DESIRED OUTCOME]
10. The Most Reliable Way to Solve [PROBLEM] (That Isn't Talked About Enough)

#### 3. Mistake & Myth-Breaking Templates
*Use to challenge bad assumptions and reset thinking.*
11. The Biggest Mistake [TARGET AUDIENCE] Makes With [TOPIC]
12. Why Most Advice About [TOPIC] Fails [TARGET AUDIENCE]
13. Stop Doing This If You Want to [DESIRED OUTCOME]
14. What [TARGET AUDIENCE] Gets Wrong About [TOPIC]
15. The Popular Strategy That Quietly Sabotages [DESIRED RESULT]

#### 4. Authority & Insight Templates
*Use to position yourself as the strategic guide.*
16. What [TOP % or EXPERIENCED GROUP] Knows About [TOPIC] That Others Don't
17. How High-Performing [TARGET AUDIENCE] Approach [TOPIC] Differently
18. The Strategic Way to Think About [TOPIC]
19. What Changes When [TARGET AUDIENCE] Stops Guessing and Starts Using a System
20. The Difference Between [AVERAGE APPROACH] and [EFFECTIVE APPROACH]

#### 5. List-Based Clarity Templates
*Use when structure and certainty increase clicks.*
21. [NUMBER] Reasons [TARGET AUDIENCE] Struggles With [PROBLEM]
22. [NUMBER] Things You Must Get Right to [DESIRED OUTCOME]
23. [NUMBER] Signals That [PROBLEM] Is Costing You More Than You Think
24. [NUMBER] Principles Behind Successful [TARGET AUDIENCE]
25. [NUMBER] Hard Truths About [TOPIC] No One Mentions

#### 6. Outcome-Driven Templates
*Use when the reader is motivated by results.*
26. How [TARGET AUDIENCE] Can Finally [DESIRED RESULT]
27. The Path From [CURRENT STATE] to [DESIRED STATE]
28. What It Takes to [DESIRED OUTCOME] Consistently
29. The Missing Step Between [EFFORT] and [RESULT]
30. What Happens When [TARGET AUDIENCE] Gets [TOPIC] Right

#### 7. Time, Ease, or Efficiency Templates
*Use when the reader wants relief or simplicity.*
31. How to [DESIRED OUTCOME] Without Overcomplicating It
32. A More Efficient Way to [COMMON TASK]
33. How to Make [TOPIC] Work Without Burning Out
34. The Simplest Path to [DESIRED RESULT]
35. Why Working Harder Isn't Fixing [PROBLEM]

#### 8. Diagnostic & Self-Identification Templates
*Use when you want readers to "see themselves" immediately.*
36. If You're [TARGET AUDIENCE], Read This Before You [COMMON ACTION]
37. Are You Accidentally Creating Your Own [PROBLEM]?
38. This One Question Reveals Why [PROBLEM] Persists
39. How to Tell If [TOPIC] Is Helping or Hurting You
40. What Your [SYMPTOM] Is Really Trying to Tell You

#### 9. Comparison & Contrast Templates
*Use to clarify decisions and positioning.*
41. The Difference Between [OPTION A] and [OPTION B]
42. Why [COMMON APPROACH] Looks Good but Underperforms
43. What Separates Successful [TARGET AUDIENCE] From the Rest
44. Why Doing More Isn't the Same as Doing What Works
45. Old Way vs. New Way: How [TOPIC] Has Changed

#### 10. Long-Form / Newsletter-Style Templates
*Use for deep thinking, authority pieces, and premium positioning.*
46. The Real Conversation About [TOPIC] No One Is Having
47. A Clear-Eyed Look at Why [PROBLEM] Keeps Happening
48. What Most People Miss When Thinking About [TOPIC]
49. The Long-Term Consequences of Ignoring [PROBLEM]
50. Why This Issue Matters More Than It Seems

---

#### HOW TO USE THESE EFFECTIVELY
1. Pick ONE primary angle (problem, outcome, authority, diagnostic, etc.)
2. Fill in the placeholders with specific language
3. Write 5–10 variations using different templates
4. Choose the headline that answers:
   - What is this about?
   - Is this for me?
   - Why should I care?

Clarity always wins.
