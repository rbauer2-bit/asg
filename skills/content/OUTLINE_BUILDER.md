## PURPOSE

Produces fully structured outlines for any content piece before writing begins. The outline specifies the argument, the evidence type, the emotional function, and the Belief-to-Buy stage pair for every section — giving any writer on the team a load-bearing document they can execute from without ambiguity.

This skill is a prerequisite for `BLOG_POST_WRITER.md`, `BELIEF_TO_BUY_BODY_WRITER.md`, `VSL_CREATOR.md`, and any other skill where structural clarity before writing is required.

### STEP 1 — COLLECT INPUTS

Before building anything, confirm you have the following. If any are missing, ask for them before proceeding:

- **Topic**: The subject of the report or guide
- **Content type**: Report / lead magnet / blog post / LinkedIn article / email sequence / VSL / landing page
- **Target audience**: Who is this for? Their role, situation, biggest frustration
- **Client context**: Niche, named services, geography (from `client-context.yaml` if available)
- **Key transformation**: What does the reader know / believe / be able to do after consuming this content that they couldn't before?
- **Number of chapters**: How many key points to cover (default: 7–10)
- **Target length**: Approximate word count or page count (if specified)

---

### STEP 2 — GENERATE THE OUTLINE

Once inputs are confirmed, produce the full outline using the structure below. Every chapter follows the same four-part framework — no exceptions. Vary wording and tone between chapters to avoid repetition while keeping the structure consistent.

---

### OUTPUT STRUCTURE

```
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
OUTLINE: [Full working title of the report or guide]
Content type: [report / lead magnet / blog post / etc.]
Target audience: [brief description]
Key transformation: [what the reader gains]
Chapters: [number]
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

CHAPTER [#]: [Chapter Title — specific, outcome-oriented, not generic]

  I. WHY? — The Motivational Foundation
  Purpose: Establish why the reader must care about this topic before anything else is said.

    1. Problem
       - What obstacles or challenges prevent the reader from achieving their goal here?
       - Why does this matter? What frustration or pain does it cause?
       - How does this problem affect others connected to the reader (team, clients, family)?

    2. Impact
       - What happens if this problem worsens or is ignored?
       - What areas of life, work, or business could be harmed?
       - Vivid picture of consequences or missed opportunities.

    3. Cause
       - The root cause beneath the surface symptom — what is overlooked or misunderstood?
       - The "aha" insight that reframes how the reader sees the problem.

    4. Three Key Motivators
       - Authority / Control: How mastering this gives the reader power over their outcome.
       - Affiliation / Connection: How it connects them to a valued group or community.
       - Accomplishment: The pride and satisfaction that comes from progress or mastery here.

    5. Dual Spectrum
       - Negative (current reality): The situation the reader wants to escape.
       - Positive (ideal state): The future they want to reach.

    6. Potential Outcomes
       - What happens if the reader acts on this chapter?
       - What is the likely result of doing nothing?
       - Build urgency and motivation to continue.

  II. WHAT? — The Knowledge Base
  Purpose: Define the solution clearly and explain how it works.

    1. Prescribed Solution
       - The recommended approach or strategy — defined in clear, plain terms.

    2. Features
       - Specific elements or characteristics of the solution.
       - What makes it different or valuable.

    3. Advantages
       - How this solution outperforms other approaches.
       - Practical edge it provides.

    4. Benefits (Emotional Payoff)
       - The deeper "why it matters" — feelings, life improvements, relief.

    5. Principles Behind the Solution
       - Supporting facts, frameworks, or evidence that explain why it works.
       - Include credible data or examples where available.

    6. Process Overview
       - High-level flow of how the solution operates from start to finish.
       - Written as a simple sequence or visual-ready flowchart description.

    7. Procedures
       - Step-by-step actions the reader must take.
       - Each step actionable and specific — no vague instructions.

  III. HOW? — The Skill Building
  Purpose: Help the reader internalize and apply the solution with confidence.

    1. Skill Building & Practice
       - Exercises, drills, or repetition strategies to develop real mastery.
       - How to practice and reinforce new habits or capabilities.

    2. Coaching & Overcoming Roadblocks
       - Common challenges, objections, or excuses that derail progress.
       - Mindsets, tools, or reframes to break through them.

    3. Using Feedback
       - How to track progress and use results as ongoing feedback.
       - How to iterate and adjust for continuous improvement.

  IV. WHAT NOW? — Next Steps & Application
  Purpose: Motivate the reader to act and show them where to apply what they've learned.

    1. Possible Outcomes
       - Specific, tangible results the reader can expect if they apply this well.
       - Future-paced: help them see their improved situation in vivid terms.

    2. Where to Use This
       - Scenarios, projects, or contexts where the reader should apply these techniques.

    3. Where NOT to Use This
       - Situations where this approach could backfire or be misapplied.
       - Common pitfalls to avoid.

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
[Repeat Chapter structure for all remaining chapters]
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

FELIX'S STRUCTURAL NOTE:
[2–4 sentences on the reasoning behind the chapter sequence, any pacing decisions,
and any watch-outs for the writer executing from this outline.]
```

### STEP 3 — WRITING GUIDELINES (apply to every chapter)

- Write each sub-point with enough specificity that the body writer knows exactly what to say — not just topic labels, but directional content notes
- Vary tone and framing between chapters to prevent repetitive structure from becoming predictable
- Use the client's specific niche, avatar language, and named services wherever possible — no generic placeholders
- The "Dual Spectrum" in every Why section must reflect the client's actual before/after — not hypothetical generics
- Evidence type for each "Principles Behind the Solution" must be identified: story / data / case example / mechanism / third-party proof
- Every "Procedures" section must be written as numbered steps — not prose descriptions
- Every chapter's "What Now?" must end with a natural forward bridge into the next chapter's topic
