## PURPOSE

Every Authority Blueprint™ includes two connected assets that work together:

**1. The Lead Magnet** — a substantive, high-value guide that an ideal prospect opts in to receive. It is the primary tool for building the email list, initiating the Welcome Sequence, and establishing credibility before the first consultation.

**2. The Referral Program** — a structured system for converting satisfied clients into active referral sources at their moment of highest satisfaction. It captures the referral impulse that currently evaporates because no system exists to direct it.

These two assets are presented together in Section 11 because they share a purpose: converting trust into pipeline. The lead magnet converts stranger trust into lead capture. The referral program converts client trust into warm introductions.

## PART 1: THE LEAD MAGNET

### SECTION A — THE STRATEGIC ROLE OF THE LEAD MAGNET

The lead magnet is not a brochure. It is not a list of FAQs. It is not a "getting to know us" document.

The lead magnet has one job: **move the ideal prospect from B1/E1 (Enemy Belief + Dissatisfaction) to B3/E3 (New Worldview + Desire) in a single sitting** — and end with a clear, low-friction path to the consultation.

A prospect who reads the lead magnet and follows through to the Welcome Sequence should arrive at their consultation already having done 80% of the belief and emotion track work. The consultation is the close, not the pitch.

---

### SECTION B — LEAD MAGNET SPECIFICATIONS

**Title formula**:
"The [Geography] [Service] [Authority Word]: [What You Need to Know Before You Make Any Decisions]"

Examples:
- "The Louisville Family Law Survival Guide: What You Need to Know Before You Make Any Decisions"
- "The Phoenix Divorce Playbook: What Every Arizona Resident Should Know Before They File"
- "The Dallas Business Owner's Divorce Guide: Protecting Your Company Before the Papers Are Served"

**Title requirements**:
- Geography-specific (not national/generic)
- Service-specific (not practice-area-general)
- Addresses the avatar at their current state (before they've decided anything — reduces barrier to opt-in)
- Promises clarity and preparation, not a sales pitch

**Format**:
- PDF, 12-18 pages
- Fully designed — branded with the niche site identity (not the main firm brand, if a niche site exists)
- Headings, subheadings, callout boxes, compliance disclaimers in footer
- Attorney/owner headshot on page 2 or 3 (builds trust immediately)
- Awards/credentials displayed once (not on every page — once earns trust, repeat feels like selling)
- CTA to free consultation on pages 6-8, 12, and final page

**Distribution**:
- Primary: Niche website opt-in form (triggers Welcome Sequence automatically)
- Secondary: Professional referral partners share with their clients (as a value-add)
- Tertiary: Past client reactivation sequence includes as value-first attachment

### SECTION C — LEAD MAGNET TABLE OF CONTENTS TEMPLATE

The TOC follows the Belief-to-Buy Framework™ arc — it takes the avatar through the full conversion journey in document form. Every section maps to a stage pair.

**Standard 10-section structure (customize titles for client and niche):**

| Section | Stage Pair | Purpose |
|---------|-----------|---------|
| 1. Before You Make Any Decisions | B1/E1 | Pattern interrupt — meets avatar where they are |
| 2. How [Local Court / Process] Actually Works | B2/E2 | Pre-frame — changes their lens before they evaluate options |
| 3. The Question Everyone Asks Wrong | B2/E2 | Addresses the Enemy Belief directly (e.g., "solo vs. big firm") |
| 4. The [X] Factors That Actually Determine Your Outcome | B3/E3 | New Worldview — the evaluation framework that favors the client |
| 5. The First [30/60] Days: What to Protect and Document | B3/E3 | Practical application of New Worldview — urgency begins here |
| 6. What [Service Type] Really Costs in [City] — And How to Control It | B3/E3 | Removes cost fear — the #1 barrier to taking action |
| 7. What Courts Actually Consider in [Case Type] Decisions | B3/E3 | Deep authority — specific to local jurisdiction |
| 8. When to Act, When to Wait, and What Your Timing Affects | B4/E4 | Internal Alignment + Urgency — the cost of delay |
| 9. What to Expect When You Call: Your Free Consultation, Explained | B5/E5 | Trust Closer — removes all unknowns about the next step |
| 10. About [Attorney Name] | B5/E5 | 17 years, named credentials, direct access — the final proof point |

**Customization instructions:**
- Section 3 title changes based on the Enemy Belief identified in the Belief Filter Map
- Section 5 and 7 are written specifically for the client's geography and jurisdiction (not national law)
- Section 8 is the urgency section — it must surface the real cost of waiting, not manufactured pressure
- Section 9 must describe the actual consultation process at this specific firm

### SECTION D — LEAD MAGNET QUALITY STANDARDS

Before the lead magnet is considered complete, apply the Belief-to-Buy™ content quality test to the full document:

- Does Section 1 enter the avatar's current state — not the firm's agenda?
- Does Section 3 name and address the Enemy Belief using the avatar's own language?
- Does Section 7 (or equivalent) make the after-state concretely visible and desirable?
- Does Section 8 surface real urgency without manufactured pressure?
- Does Section 9 make the consultation feel safe and obvious, not a sales pitch?
- Is every section geography-specific? Could it be used by an attorney in a different city without significant rewriting? (If yes — rewrite with local specificity until no.)

**Length note**: 12-18 pages is the target. Under 12 pages = not enough authority-building depth. Over 20 pages = completion rate drops and the welcome sequence loses its value.

## PART 2: THE REFERRAL PROGRAM FRAMEWORK

### SECTION E — THE STRATEGIC ROLE OF THE REFERRAL PROGRAM

Professional service businesses get referrals by accident. ASG clients get referrals by design.

The referral program is a structured system that does three things:
1. **Captures the referral impulse** at the moment of highest satisfaction (right after a positive outcome or a 4/5-star review)
2. **Directs it** — gives the client a specific, easy action to take
3. **Reinforces it** — makes referring feel good and worth repeating

The Referral Capture email sequence (Sequence 2 in Section 10) is the operational delivery mechanism. This section specifies the program structure, the incentive design, and the compliance mechanics.

---

### SECTION F — REFERRAL PROGRAM STRUCTURE

**The trigger moment**: The referral ask is most effective within 24-72 hours of:
- A matter closing successfully
- A 4 or 5-star Google review being confirmed
- A client expressing satisfaction directly (email, phone, in person)

The Referral Capture email sequence (see EMAIL_SEQUENCES.md Sequence 2) automates this trigger for online reviews. For direct expressions of satisfaction, the referral ask is a trained response by the attorney/owner or intake staff.

**The ask format** (three options, in order of effectiveness):

Option A — The pre-written text (highest conversion):
Give the client a text message or email they can copy-paste and send immediately. Remove all friction from the introduction.
```
"Hey [Name] — I worked with [Attorney Name] at [Firm] and she was incredible.
Honestly worth calling: [phone number]. Tell her [Client First Name] sent you."
```

Option B — The forward-this-email:
The Referral Capture Email 3 (see EMAIL_SEQUENCES.md) includes a pre-written forwarding message. One click.

Option C — The direct introduction reply:
"Reply with their name and contact info and I'll reach out personally." Most personal, but lowest conversion — requires the referrer to do more work.

All three options are presented in the referral sequence. The client chooses the one that feels comfortable.

---

### SECTION G — INCENTIVE DESIGN AND COMPLIANCE

**Non-financial incentives** (required when state bar rules prohibit referral fees to non-attorneys):

The non-financial thank-you acknowledges the referral without creating a transactional arrangement. Options:

- Personal handwritten note from the attorney (highest perceived value, lowest cost)
- Gift card to a local restaurant or retailer ($50-$100 — from the attorney personally, not as a firm policy)
- Charitable donation in the referrer's name to a cause relevant to the niche (e.g., Kentucky Domestic Violence Association for family law; Habitat for Humanity for real estate; local food bank for general services)
- Recognition in a client newsletter or social post (for clients who give consent)

**The charitable donation option** is recommended for professional service niches with referral fee restrictions because:
- It creates a memorable, shareable moment (referrer tells the story)
- It positions the firm as community-minded (authority reinforcement)
- It is unambiguously non-transactional
- It is fully compliant in all professional service bars

**Attorney referral fee structures** (when the referral source is another attorney):
- Apply jurisdiction-specific rules (e.g., Kentucky Rule 1.5(e) requires client consent and written agreement)
- Include in the referral program only when the referral source pool includes attorneys
- Do not apply to client, CPA, therapist, or other non-attorney referral sources

**Pull compliance_flags from niche-library/[niche-id].yaml before finalizing incentive design.** Any restriction that affects the incentive structure must be reflected in the program design — not just noted as a caveat.

### SECTION H — PROFESSIONAL REFERRAL NETWORK EXTENSION

Beyond past clients, every professional service firm has a natural professional referral ecosystem — adjacent professionals who serve the same clients in complementary capacities.

Identify the top 3-5 professional referral source categories for this niche from the niche library `fast_cash_strategy_fit.strategy_11_partner_marketing.notes`. Examples:

| Niche | Professional Referral Sources |
|-------|------------------------------|
| Family law | CPAs, financial advisors, therapists, DV organizations, real estate attorneys |
| Personal injury | Emergency physicians, chiropractors, physical therapists, auto body shops |
| Dental | ENTs, oral surgeons, orthodontists, primary care physicians |
| Financial advisory | Estate attorneys, CPAs, HR directors at local employers, insurance agents |

For each category, specify:
- How many outreach contacts to target in the first 90 days
- The outreach mechanism (Sequence 4 in Section 10 — Professional Referral Partner Outreach)
- What the firm offers the referral source (the Louisville Family Law Survival Guide equivalent — something valuable to share with their clients)
- What the referral arrangement looks like in practice (how introductions are made, what to tell clients, what to expect)

**Professional referral partner welcome kit** (produced separately from the Blueprint, in Q2):
- One-page "Who we work best with" brief
- The lead magnet (shareable resource for their clients)
- Instructions for how to make an introduction
- Direct contact for Anna / the attorney (not the general intake line)
