## PURPOSE

Build the 13-week content calendar for the current quarter's focus service. Every week's content is deliberately sequenced against the Belief-to-Buy™ dual track — moving the avatar forward, not just filling a publishing schedule.

The 13-week structure is designed so that a cold prospect who enters the content ecosystem in Week 1 and follows the full sequence arrives at the consultation conversation already at B4/E4 (Internal Alignment + Urgency) — doing the heavy persuasion work before the sales conversation even starts.

## THE 13-WEEK ARCHITECTURE

The quarter is divided into three phases:

**WEEKS 1-4: BELIEF DISRUPTION PHASE** (B1/E1 → B2/E2)
Primary goal: Break the Enemy Belief, surface dissatisfaction, create contrast.
Avatar enters at: Normalizing their problem, operating on wrong assumptions.
Avatar exits at: Their existing interpretation has been challenged, a new frame is available.
Content tone: Empathetic, pattern-interrupting, non-salesy.
Primary format: High-reach content (social, short video, hooks that stop scrolling).

**WEEKS 5-9: AUTHORITY BUILDING PHASE** (B2/E2 → B3/E3 → B4/E4)
Primary goal: Build the New Worldview, produce Internal Alignment, surface Desire and Urgency.
Avatar enters at: Open to a new frame, wanting to understand better.
Avatar exits at: They understand the problem deeply, can explain past failures, want the outcome, and feel the cost of delay.
Content tone: Teaching, story-driven, outcome-specific.
Primary format: Depth content (long-form articles, videos, email sequences, case studies).

**WEEKS 10-13: CONVERSION PHASE** (B4/E4 → B5/E5 → BUY)
Primary goal: Produce Certainty and Relief simultaneously. Make the consultation the obvious next step.
Avatar enters at: Convinced intellectually, feeling urgency, but still carrying final objection.
Avatar exits at: The last friction is removed. Action feels safe and obvious.
Content tone: Transparent, process-forward, de-risking.
Primary format: Trust content (consultations, FAQ, what to expect, case study close, direct offer).

## EXECUTION INSTRUCTIONS

### Phase 1 — Pull the Stage Sequence

From the Belief Filter Map Master Content Map table, extract the content pieces assigned to each stage pair. These are the backbone of the calendar — every content piece has its stage pair assignment before we schedule it.

### Phase 2 — Layer the Distribution Channels

For each week, assign content across the distribution channels active for this client (from `client-context.yaml current_marketing_channels` and content format priorities from CONTENT_STRATEGY.md).

Standard weekly cadence (adjust based on client's team capacity):
- 3-5 social posts (short-form, high-frequency)
- 1 long-form anchor piece (blog/video/email — the week's primary stage-pair mover)
- 1 email to list (if active list exists)
- 1 Google Business post (local SEO + review builder)

### Phase 4 — Seasonal Adjustment

After building the raw calendar, cross-reference with `niche-library/[niche-id].yaml seasonal_patterns`.

If a seasonal peak falls within the quarter:
- Advance Phase 3 (Conversion) content by 1-2 weeks to front-run the peak
- Add additional Trust Closer (B5/E5) content in the 2 weeks before the peak
- Schedule the Week 13 direct offer to land at peak season entry

If the quarter is in a slow period:
- Invest more heavily in Phase 2 (Authority Building) — prospects are researching, not buying
- Use slow months for case study production and anchor piece development
- Phase 3 content prepares prospects to act when peak season arrives
