- Be written in a conversational but authoritative tone
- Feel natural and trust-building — never robotic, never salesy
- Maintain strong retention using psychological storytelling across its full duration

- End with a compelling but natural CTA: **[Call to Action]**

### REQUIRED STRUCTURE

**Section 1 — Hook (0:00–0:30)**

Open with tension, a costly mistake, or a common misconception.

- Avoid generic intros ("Hey guys, welcome back")
- Make the viewer feel this is directly relevant to their situation
- Create a knowledge gap — promise something they don't know yet
- Clearly state what they will learn by watching to the end

The hook earns the next 30 seconds. Every word must pull the viewer forward.

---

**Section 2 — Authority Positioning (Brief and Integrated)**

Establish credibility without a dedicated "about me" block.

- Mention volume of work, years of local experience, or a specific result
- Weave it into the narrative — do not stop the video to introduce yourself
- Keep it to 2–3 sentences maximum
- Tie it directly to why this topic matters and why you are the right person to discuss it

---

**Section 3 — Core Content (3–5 Sections)**

This is the educational payload. Build it in clearly labeled sections, each structured as:

1. **Introduce the insight or mistake** — name it plainly
2. **Explain why it matters** — financially, emotionally, or practically for [Target Customer Avatar]
3. **Use a real-world example** — specific to [Specific Service + Location] and [Target Customer Avatar]
4. **Address the internal objection** — what the viewer is thinking right now that might make them dismiss this
5. **Transition naturally** — bridge to the next insight without hard breaks

Do not deliver content as a bullet list. Write it as a guided conversation between an expert and a homeowner sitting across the table. Sentence length varies. Pacing varies. Some sentences are very short. Others are longer and build toward a point. That rhythm is intentional.

---

**Section 4 — Mid-Video Re-Engagement**

At approximately the midpoint of the script:

- Re-anchor the stakes: remind the viewer what they risk by not understanding this
- Reinforce why this decision matters more than they may have realized
- Increase urgency slightly — not artificial, but earned through the content already delivered
- This section should feel like a natural pause that re-focuses the viewer, not an interruption

One paragraph is sufficient. Do not break the narrative.

---

**Section 5 — Final Insight**

Deliver a strong, memorable takeaway.

- Reframe the entire topic in one clear idea
- Make it feel like the viewer just received something valuable
- Leave them feeling smarter, more confident, and better equipped
- This is not a summary — it is a conclusion that earns the CTA

---

**Section 6 — Call to Action**

Tie the CTA directly to the pain and desire established in the hook.

- Make the next step feel logical and easy — not high-pressure
- Avoid hype and exaggeration
- Reference the specific service and location where appropriate
- One CTA only — do not stack multiple asks

---

### STORYTELLING CONSTRAINTS

- No hedging words: avoid "maybe," "might," "could," "probably," "I think"
- Speak with calm certainty throughout
- Use contrast continuously: what people commonly believe vs. what is actually true
- Address unspoken viewer concerns directly — do not wait for them to object
- Vary sentence length for rhythm — short punches mixed with longer explanatory sentences
- No bullet points in the final script
- No filler phrases ("so," "basically," "right?")
- Clear paragraph spacing formatted for teleprompter use
- Write for the ear, not the eye — read every line aloud before finalizing
