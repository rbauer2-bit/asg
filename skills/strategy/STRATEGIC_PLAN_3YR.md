## PURPOSE

Produce the Year 2-3 strategic growth arc. This section answers the question every ambitious business owner has but rarely asks their marketing agency: *"If this works, what does the next level actually look like?"*

The 3-year arc transforms the Authority Blueprint™ from a marketing plan into a business growth roadmap. It shows the client they're not just buying campaigns — they're building something that compounds.

**Tone shift from Year 1**: Year 1 is tactical and specific. Years 2-3 shift toward strategic and architectural. The client is moving from "building the system" to "scaling what works."

## EXECUTION INSTRUCTIONS

### SECTION 5A — The Year 2-3 Strategic Thesis

**Voice**: Daniel Frost — visionary but grounded

Write a 2-paragraph framing for the 2-3 year horizon:

**Paragraph 1 — What Year 1 Makes Possible**:
By the end of Year 1, [Client Name] should have [specific assets built]. These assets are the precondition for what becomes possible in Years 2-3. Name them specifically:
- A live authority content library in [specific niche area] that is ranking and driving organic leads
- A referral network with [professional partner type] relationships generating consistent introductions
- A reputation with [X] Google reviews at [X.X] rating that pre-sells new prospects
- A customer base that has experienced the retention sequence and is generating repeat/modification work
- Conversion data: which content, which campaigns, which messages actually close clients

That foundation makes Years 2-3 a fundamentally different operation than Year 1.

**Paragraph 2 — The Year 2-3 Opportunity**:
What becomes available once that foundation exists? This should be specific to the client's niche and geography:
- Can they expand geographically? (Second location, additional metro area)
- Can they add a service line that leverages the authority built in Year 1?
- Can they raise prices on the strength of a visible authority position?
- Can they move from paid acquisition dependent to primarily organic/referral?
- Can they begin grooming the business for acquisition or partnership?

---

### SECTION 5B — Year 2 Strategic Priorities

**Voice**: Daniel Frost

Structure Year 2 around 3-4 strategic priorities (not a month-by-month plan — that's Year 1 territory):

**Priority 1 — Scale What Works**:
Identify the 2-3 channels or strategies from Year 1 that are performing best. Year 2's first priority is increasing investment in those, not launching new experiments.

Frame as: "Based on Year 1 performance data, [Channel/Strategy X] is expected to be the most productive acquisition lever. Year 2 invests [specific increase] in this channel and optimizes the conversion path it feeds into."

**Priority 2 — Geographic or Service Expansion** (if applicable):
Is there a logical expansion move in Year 2? This should be tied to the market opportunity analysis — is there an adjacent geography or service line where the Year 1 authority position is directly transferable?

If expansion is not the right move (business isn't ready, market doesn't support it) — say so explicitly. Whitfield's voice is useful here: "Growth for its own sake without the systems to support it destroys enterprise value faster than stagnation."

**Priority 3 — Authority Deepening**:
Year 1 established the position. Year 2 fortifies it. This means:
- Case studies published and actively promoted
- Speaking, media, or publication opportunities in the vertical
- Professional association or recognition programs that add third-party credibility
- Potential book, guide, or signature intellectual property

**Priority 4 — Retention Economics**:
By Year 2, there's a meaningful client base to work with. Year 2 should show a measurable improvement in:
- Client LTV (from upsell/cross-sell systems built in Year 1)
- Referral rate (from the referral program maturing)
- Reactivation rate (from past clients returning for additional services)

Include James Whitfield III's lens on this priority:
> *"A 10% improvement in retention rate at [Client Name]'s Year 2 revenue level is worth approximately $[calculated amount] in additional annual revenue — with zero additional acquisition cost. Retention work in Year 2 has a better ROI than acquisition work at this stage."*

### SECTION 5C — Year 3 Vision & Scale Inflection

**Voice**: Daniel Frost for strategy, James Whitfield III for the financial/value framing

Year 3 is written at a higher altitude. The specific tactics are not yet determined — they depend on Year 1-2 performance. What IS determined is the strategic outcome Year 3 should produce.

**The Year 3 Position Statement**:
A single paragraph describing where [Client Name] sits in their market at the end of Year 3 if the plan executes. This should be ambitious but specific:

> "By the end of Year 3, [Client Name] is the recognized authority in [specific niche/geography] — meaning that when a [professional referral type] needs to refer a client for [service], [Client Name] is the first name that comes to mind. New prospects arrive pre-sold on the brand. Consultations close at a higher rate than Year 1 because the authority position is doing the early persuasion work. The business has [X] reviews, ranks on the first page for [Y] high-intent search terms, and generates [Z%] of new clients through referral and organic channels."

**The Financial Picture** (James Whitfield III voice):

Build a simple 3-year revenue model:

```
Year 1 Revenue Target:    $[X]
Year 2 Revenue Target:    $[Y]  (+[%] growth)
Year 3 Revenue Target:    $[Z]  (+[%] growth)

Key growth levers:
  - Transaction volume increase: [X new clients/year]
  - Average transaction value increase: [X%] from authority pricing
  - LTV improvement: [X%] from retention systems
  - Referral-driven acquisition: [X%] of new clients by Year 3

At Year 3 revenue of $[Z], with [estimated margin]% EBITDA margin,
this business carries an estimated value of $[Z × multiple] — based on
[industry standard multiple for this niche].

That's not an incidental outcome. It's a designed outcome.
```

**The Scale Decision Point**:
Name the 2-3 decision points that will define Year 3's trajectory. These are the forks in the road — the moments where the business chooses its path:
- Geographic expansion vs. service depth
- Hiring to scale vs. optimizing the current team for higher output
- Owner-dependent model vs. building transferable systems
- Continued independence vs. exploring strategic partnership or acquisition

Flag these as decisions to revisit at the Year 2 review, not decisions to make now.
