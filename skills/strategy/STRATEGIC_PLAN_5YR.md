## PURPOSE

The 5-year vision is not a tactical plan. It's an ownership argument.

By Year 5, the client has a choice: run a highly profitable, well-positioned professional service business for maximum income — OR own an asset that a strategic buyer, private equity group, or partner would pay a significant multiple to acquire.

Both are valid. The 5-year section of the Blueprint makes sure the client knows both paths are available, and that the marketing and systems work ASG does in Years 1-3 is the same work that builds enterprise value for either outcome.

This section is what closes the mental gap between "I'm hiring a marketing agency" and "I'm investing in the long-term value of my business."

### SECTION 6D — The 5-Year Decision Framework

**Voice**: Daniel Frost

At Year 5, [Client Name] faces a strategic decision. Present the three primary paths and their implications:

**Path 1 — Maximum Income Operation**
Keep the business, optimize for profitability and owner income. Year 5 systems produce revenue with minimal owner dependency. The authority position means premium pricing is sustainable. This is a highly rewarding long-term operation.

*What this path requires from now*: Systems designed for owner independence, team development, documented processes.

**Path 2 — Strategic Scale**
Use Year 5 position as the launchpad for geographic expansion, franchise model, or multi-location operation. The authority model replicates to new markets using the same framework.

*What this path requires from now*: Documented systems, replicable content model, brand standards that transfer.

**Path 3 — Strategic Transaction**
Sell to a strategic buyer (larger firm, private equity, consolidator) at a premium multiple. Year 5 authority position and systems make this business highly acquisable.

*What this path requires from now*: Clean financials, documented systems, a brand that transfers (not just the owner's personal relationships), a referral network that survives ownership change.

Flag clearly: ASG's approach is designed to produce the best possible outcome regardless of which path the client chooses. The work done in Year 1 doesn't need to be redone if they decide in Year 4 they want a transaction instead of a scale play.
