## EXECUTION INSTRUCTIONS

### Section 3A — Competitive Landscape Analysis

**Voice**: Daniel Frost, CSO — structured, confident, evidence-forward

For each of the top 3 competitors listed in `client-context.yaml`:

1. **Profile the competitor** (3-4 sentences):
   - What they're known for / how they position themselves
   - What they do well (be honest — don't understate real strengths)
   - Their most visible weakness from a buyer's perspective
   - Specific evidence of the weakness (thin review volume, no content, poor website positioning, generic messaging, slow response, etc.)

2. **Grade their authority presence** across 5 dimensions:
   - Website positioning clarity (do you know in 5 seconds who they serve and what outcome they deliver?)
   - Review volume and velocity (are they actively generating social proof?)
   - Content and education (are they teaching, or just broadcasting?)
   - Local search visibility (are they appearing for intent-based local searches?)
   - Referral network (visible professional referral relationships?)
   
   Grade each: Strong / Moderate / Weak / Absent

3. **The competitive gap summary** (1 paragraph after all 3 profiles):
   > "Of the three primary competitors in [City] [Niche] market, none currently occupies the position of [specific authority claim]. [Competitor 1] leads on [dimension] but has [specific weakness]. [Competitor 2] generates [review volume/presence] but lacks [missing element]. [Competitor 3] is relatively invisible outside of [channel]. The position of [specific unclaimed claim] is available and uncontested."

### Section 3B — Market Opportunity Analysis

**Voice**: Daniel Frost — analytical, specific numbers

1. **Market size narrative** (not a number — a story):
   - How many people in this geography are in the active search window for this service annually?
   - What percentage of them hire anyone vs. solve the problem another way?
   - What percentage go to each of the top competitors vs. smaller firms vs. the client currently?
   
   Frame as: "In [City/Metro], approximately [X] [niche-relevant trigger events] occur each year. Of these, roughly [Y%] result in a professional service engagement. [Client Name] is currently capturing an estimated [Z%] of that addressable market. The gap represents approximately [dollar amount] in revenue that is currently going elsewhere."

2. **Revenue opportunity sizing**:
   Build a simple model using client context data:
   - Current monthly client acquisitions × avg transaction value = current monthly revenue from this service
   - If capture rate improved by [10% / 25% / 50%] what does that mean in revenue?
   - Present as conservative / target / stretch scenario
   - Label clearly: these are directional estimates, not guarantees

3. **The timing argument**:
   Why is NOW the right time for this client to move? Use niche library seasonal patterns + current competitive landscape gaps. If there's a window closing (competitor about to invest in marketing, seasonal peak approaching, market shift) — name it.

### Section 3C — Ideal Avatar Profile

**Voice**: Dr. Raymond Cross, Board — behavioral science lens, precise and warm

This section presents the ideal avatar to the client in a way that makes them say "that's exactly right."

Format as a narrative profile, not a bulleted demographics list:

```
[AVATAR NAME]: [DESCRIPTIVE SUBTITLE]

THE SETUP:
[2-3 sentences describing where the avatar is in their life when they first encounter
this problem. Their circumstances. Their identity. What they care about.]

WHAT'S HAPPENING TO THEM:
[2-3 sentences describing the trigger event and their emotional experience of it.
Use their language — not clinical language.]

WHAT THEY'RE AFRAID OF:
[3-4 specific fears, written in first person as the avatar would think them]
"I'm worried that..."
"What if..."
"I've heard that..."

WHAT THEY ACTUALLY WANT:
[3-4 specific desires, in first person]
"I just want..."
"More than anything, I need..."

HOW THEY'RE SEARCHING:
[What they type into Google. What they ask their friends. Where they look for credibility.]

WHERE THEY GET STUCK:
[The belief barrier — in their own words. What story are they telling themselves
that keeps them from taking the next step?]

WHAT UNLOCKS THEM:
[The specific message, moment, or experience that moves them from stuck to action.
This is the strategic insight — the thing [Client Name]'s marketing must deliver.]
```

After the narrative, add the **Avatar Decision Framework**:
- What they notice first when evaluating a provider
- What immediately eliminates a provider from consideration
- What single factor, if present, closes the decision in [Client Name]'s favor

### Section 3D — Positioning Gap Analysis & Authority Statement

**Voice**: Daniel Frost + Vivienne Carr collaboration — strategic and creative

This is the most important paragraph in the entire strategic plan.

It names the specific position in the market that is available, explains why it's available, and claims it for the client.

Format:

**The Unclaimed Position**:
> "In [City] [Niche] market, there is no provider who credibly occupies the position of [specific, ownable claim]. Every major competitor positions on [generic dimension — credentials, years, size]. None positions on [what clients actually care about most — named specifically]."

**Why [Client Name] Can Own It**:
> "[Client Name] has [specific capability, experience, or perspective] that makes this position credible. This isn't marketing spin — it's a genuine operational advantage that [Competitor 1] and [Competitor 2] demonstrably lack."

**The Authority Statement** (this becomes the client's north star positioning):
> "[Client Name] is the [specific descriptor] [niche] in [geography] for [specific avatar] who [specific situation] and want [specific outcome] without [specific fear]."

This statement is tested against three criteria:
1. Could a competitor claim this tomorrow by just changing their website? (If yes → make it more specific)
2. Does it immediately resonate with the primary avatar? (Read it from their perspective)
3. Can [Client Name] actually deliver on this promise? (If no → revise)
