## PHASE 3: 8-WEEK LAUNCH TIMELINE

### Weeks 1–2: Foundation

**Objective:** Lock in positioning, build assets, set up infrastructure.

**Tasks:**
- [ ] Define launch positioning statement: "For [TARGET] who [PROBLEM], [SERVICE] is a [CATEGORY] that [KEY BENEFIT]. Unlike [ALTERNATIVE], we [DIFFERENTIATOR]."
- [ ] Create internal launch brief (one-pager)
- [ ] Set up landing page / waitlist page
- [ ] Set up analytics and tracking (UTM parameters, conversion goals)
- [ ] Create launch-specific email list/segment
- [ ] Draft all email sequences
- [ ] Brief on visual assets needed
- [ ] Identify 10–20 potential beta clients or early access candidates
- [ ] List 20+ communities/networks where target audience gathers

**Deliverables:** Positioning statement, landing page live, email sequences drafted, beta list built

### Weeks 3–4: Pre-Launch Warming

**Objective:** Build anticipation, grow the list, prime existing audience.

**Tasks:**
- [ ] Start teaser content (LinkedIn posts, email hints, social stories)
- [ ] Recruit and brief beta clients/testimonials
- [ ] Send "early access" email to existing list
- [ ] Reach out directly to dream 10 ideal clients
- [ ] Publish authority content related to the launch topic
- [ ] Begin collecting testimonials from beta users
- [ ] Finalize all launch assets

**Content Themes:** Problem awareness, "something big is coming," authority signal posts

### Weeks 5–6: Open Cart / Active Launch

**Objective:** Drive the primary conversion action.

**Day 1 — Launch Day:**
- Email 1: "It's here" — announce and link
- Social post: Launch announcement with story
- Direct outreach: Personal messages to warm contacts

**Day 3:**
- Email 2: Case study / results story
- Social: Testimonial or client story

**Day 5:**
- Email 3: FAQ / objection handling
- Social: Behind-the-scenes or process content

**Day 7:**
- Email 4: Last chance (if limited intake or launch deadline)
- Social: Urgency content (close of enrollment, price increase, capacity)

### Weeks 7–8: Post-Launch

**Objective:** Onboard new clients, capture learnings, plan next cycle.

**Tasks:**
- [ ] Send onboarding sequence to new clients
- [ ] Send thank-you and "closed" message to the full list
- [ ] Document key metrics (revenue, conversion rates, list growth)
- [ ] Collect testimonials from new clients
- [ ] Plan for waitlist follow-up or next launch cycle

## PHASE 4: LAUNCH EMAIL SEQUENCES

### Pre-Launch Sequence (3–4 emails)

```
Email 1 (Week 3): TEASER
  Subject: "Something I've been working on..."
  Content: Plant the seed. Hint at what's coming. Create curiosity.

Email 2 (Week 4): PROBLEM AGITATION
  Subject: "The [problem] most [audience] don't talk about"
  Content: Speak to the core pain. No pitch — pure empathy and understanding.

Email 3 (Week 5, Day Before Launch): ANNOUNCEMENT
  Subject: "Tomorrow: [Service Name] opens"
  Content: Officially announce. Build excitement. One clear CTA to landing page.
```

### Launch Sequence (4–5 emails)

```
Email 1 (Launch Day): IT'S HERE
  Subject: "[Service Name] is now open"
  Content: Full value framing, social proof, clear CTA.

Email 2 (Day 3): SOCIAL PROOF
  Subject: "How [client/situation] achieved [result]"
  Content: Case study or testimonial. Specific outcomes.

Email 3 (Day 5): OBJECTION HANDLING
  Subject: "Your questions about [Service Name], answered"
  Content: FAQ format. Address the top 3–5 objections.

Email 4 (Day 7 or Close Date): LAST CHANCE
  Subject: "[X] spots left — [Service Name] closes [day]"
  Content: Urgency. Recap key benefits. Remove all remaining friction.
```
