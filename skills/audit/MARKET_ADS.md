## PHASE 2: PLATFORM-SPECIFIC AD GENERATION

### 2.1 Google Ads (Search / RSA)

Character limits:
- Headlines: Up to 15 headlines, 30 characters each
- Descriptions: Up to 4 descriptions, 90 characters each

Generate at least 10 headlines covering:
- Brand + value prop | Pain point + solution | Specific benefit + number | Social proof | Urgency/offer | Question | How-to | Comparison | Feature-focused | Action-oriented

Generate 4 descriptions covering:
- Value proposition + CTA | Features + benefits | Social proof + trust | Urgency + offer details

Include keyword strategy: 10–15 high-intent keywords per ad group, 3–5 ad groups by theme, negative keywords list.

### 2.2 Meta Ads (Facebook + Instagram)

Generate for each ad concept:
- Primary text (3 variations: short, medium, long)
- Headline (5 variations)
- Description (3 variations)

**Ad Copy Angles (generate 5–10 per campaign):**

```
PAIN POINT: "Tired of [specific frustration]? [Solution] eliminates [pain]..."
SOCIAL PROOF: "[Number] [audience] already [benefit]. See why..."
BEFORE/AFTER: "Before [solution]: [painful state]. After: [desired state]."
OBJECTION HANDLING: "Think [product type] is [objection]? [Counter]."
URGENCY/SCARCITY: "[Limited offer]. [Number] spots left this month."
CURIOSITY: "The [industry] insight that [result] (most [audience] miss this)."
DIRECT BENEFIT: "Get [specific outcome] in [timeframe]. No [common objection]."
COMPARISON: "Still using [old way]? [Solution] gives you [advantage]."
TESTIMONIAL: "'[Specific quote about specific result]' — [Name], [title]"
HOW-TO: "How to [desired outcome] in 3 steps: 1. [Step] 2. [Step] 3. [Result]"
```

### 2.3 LinkedIn Ads

Best for: Professional services, B2B, agencies

Character limits:
- Sponsored Content: Intro text 600 chars, Headline 200 chars

LinkedIn-Specific Angles:
- Professional development: "Level up your [capability]"
- ROI-focused: "Firms using [approach] see [X]% improvement in [metric]"
- Peer comparison: "Your competitors are already using [approach]. Are you?"
- Thought leadership: "[Research/guide] reveals [surprising finding]"

Targeting to recommend:
- Job title (decision makers)
- Company size
- Industry
- Seniority level
- Matched audiences (website retargeting, email lists)

## PHASE 3: RETARGETING FUNNEL

### 3.1 Three-Stage Structure

```
STAGE 1: AWARENESS (Cold Audience — 40% of budget)
  Goal: Introduce brand and value proposition
  Audience: Lookalikes, interest-based, broad targeting
  Ad Type: Educational content, thought leadership

STAGE 2: CONSIDERATION (Warm Audience — 35% of budget)
  Goal: Build trust and handle objections
  Audience: Website visitors (7–30 days), video viewers, social engagers
  Ad Type: Case studies, testimonials, demos, comparisons

STAGE 3: CONVERSION (Hot Audience — 25% of budget)
  Goal: Drive the final conversion action
  Audience: Pricing page visitors, high-intent page visitors, inquiry abandonments
  Ad Type: Direct offer, urgency, guarantee, limited-time
```

### 3.2 Budget Allocation by Business Type

| Business Type | Google | Meta | LinkedIn |
|---|---|---|---|
| Professional Services | 20% | 30% | 35% |
| SaaS (B2B) | 30% | 25% | 30% |
| Local Business | 50% | 35% | 0% |

## PHASE 4: TESTING FRAMEWORK

**Test Priority Order:**
1. Audience (who you target)
2. Offer (free consultation vs. lead magnet vs. demo)
3. Creative concept (the big idea)
4. Headline (specific wording)
5. Body copy
6. CTA

**Testing Rules:**
- Test one variable at a time
- Run tests for at least 3–5 days or 1,000 impressions per variant
- Kill underperformers at 2x the CPA target
- Scale winners by 20% budget increments
