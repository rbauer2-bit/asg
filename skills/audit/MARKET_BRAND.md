## Authority Systems Group™ — Digital Intelligence Division

## PHASE 1: BRAND VOICE DISCOVERY

### 1.1 Fetch and Analyze

Use WebFetch to retrieve:
- Homepage (primary voice expression)
- About page (brand personality and story)
- Blog or content section (voice in practice)
- Any email copy or social media linked from the site
- Testimonials and case study language (how clients describe them)

### 1.2 Voice Dimension Analysis

Score each dimension on a 1–5 scale and describe the position:

| Dimension | Scale | Description |
|---|---|---|
| Formality | 1 (Very Casual) → 5 (Very Formal) | How professionally structured is the language? |
| Emotion | 1 (Very Neutral) → 5 (Very Passionate) | How much feeling comes through in the writing? |
| Complexity | 1 (Very Simple) → 5 (Very Technical) | How sophisticated is the vocabulary and sentence structure? |
| Humor | 1 (Very Serious) → 5 (Very Playful) | Is there levity, wit, or jokes? |
| Authority | 1 (Peer/Friend) → 5 (Expert/Institution) | Does it position as an expert or as a relatable peer? |
| Directness | 1 (Indirect/Soft) → 5 (Blunt/Direct) | Does it speak plainly and get to the point quickly? |

### 1.3 Language Pattern Analysis

Identify:
- **Signature phrases** — words or constructions used repeatedly
- **Vocabulary patterns** — formal or informal? Industry-specific or accessible?
- **Sentence length** — long complex sentences or short punchy ones?
- **Point of view** — first person (I/we), second person (you), third person?
- **Headline style** — benefit-led, question-led, statement-led?
- **CTA style** — commanding, inviting, aspirational?

### 1.4 Brand Personality Archetypes

Identify the 1–2 dominant archetypes:

| Archetype | Traits | Examples |
|---|---|---|
| **Expert/Sage** | Knowledgeable, analytical, authoritative | "Here's what the data shows..." |
| **Guide/Mentor** | Supportive, encouraging, empowering | "We help you..." |
| **Hero/Champion** | Bold, direct, results-focused | "We get results. Period." |
| **Partner/Friend** | Casual, warm, collaborative | "Let's figure this out together." |
| **Innovator/Rebel** | Challenging norms, contrarian, fresh | "Ignore the playbook. Here's what works." |
| **Trusted Advisor** | Credible, measured, long-view | "Based on 20 years in this industry..." |

## PHASE 2: VOICE EFFECTIVENESS EVALUATION

### 2.1 Does the Voice Match the Audience?

Evaluate:
- Is the tone appropriate for the target client's sophistication level?
- Does the formality level match how the ideal client speaks?
- Is the authority level appropriate (not too academic, not too casual)?

### 2.2 Is the Voice Consistent?

Check across pages:
- Does the homepage, about page, and blog feel like the same brand?
- Are there jarring shifts between formal and informal?
- Are there sections that clearly weren't written by the same voice?

### 2.3 Is the Voice Differentiated?

- Could this copy be on any competitor's website?
- Are there specific phrases or perspectives that are uniquely this brand?
- What's the most memorable or distinctive thing about how they write?
