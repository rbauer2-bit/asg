## THE BELIEF-TO-BUY FRAMEWORK™

Every piece of content must move the reader through one specific belief stage. Do not try to hit multiple stages in one piece.

```
Stage 1 — Unaware
  Reader doesn't know they have a problem.
  Goal: Create pattern interrupt. Surface a hidden cost or consequence.
  Tone: Observational, curious, non-threatening.

Stage 2 — Problem Aware
  Reader knows something is wrong but hasn't named it.
  Goal: Name the problem precisely. Make them feel seen.
  Tone: Empathetic, validating, direct.

Stage 3 — Solution Aware
  Reader knows solutions exist but doesn't know which one is right.
  Goal: Educate on the category. Position your approach as different.
  Tone: Educational, confident, non-salesy.

Stage 4 — Product Aware
  Reader knows about you but hasn't decided.
  Goal: Eliminate objections. Reduce perceived risk. Build certainty.
  Tone: Evidence-based, reassuring, specific.

Stage 5 — Most Aware
  Reader is ready but needs a reason to act now.
  Goal: Create urgency. Make the next step feel obvious and easy.
  Tone: Direct, momentum-building, low pressure.

Stage 6 — Customer / Referral
  Reader is already a client.
  Goal: Reinforce the decision. Invite referral. Build loyalty.
  Tone: Warm, affirming, community-building.
```

## STORYTELLING CONSTRUCTION RULES

**The Daily Hook Integration Method:**

1. Open with the celebration or holiday as a human story — not an announcement
2. Find the metaphorical bridge between the celebration and the avatar's situation
3. Transition naturally into the belief stage content
4. Never make the celebration feel forced or bolted on
5. The reader should feel the connection before they're told what it is

**Example Bridge Logic:**
- Festival of Sleep Day + HVAC niche → "Bad sleep is often a temperature problem. Here's what most homeowners don't realize about their nighttime air quality..."
- National Chocolate Covered Cherry Day + Family Law → "The best things are often hidden under a surface layer. Your legal rights in a divorce are the same way..."
- World Kindness Week + Implant Dentistry → "Being kind to yourself starts with what you see in the mirror every morning..."

**Monthly Symbol Usage:**
- Use birthstone or flower as a secondary texture element, not a primary hook
- Works best in long-form and email — lighter touch in social
- Example: "March's birthstone is aquamarine — associated with clarity and calm. Both things you deserve when making a decision this big."

---

## WRITING CONSTRAINTS

Apply across all content types:

- No hedging language — eliminate: maybe, might, could, probably, it depends
- Speak with calm certainty
- Use contrast — what people assume vs. what is actually true
- Address unspoken objections directly
- Vary sentence length deliberately — short for impact, long for explanation
- No bullet points in final prose output
- No filler phrases: "Great question," "As I mentioned," "In today's world"
- No robotic transitions: "Moving on," "Next up," "In conclusion"
- Write as a trusted expert having a conversation, not a marketer running a campaign

## OUTPUT SPECIFICATIONS BY CONTENT TYPE

---

### SOCIAL POST (Facebook / LinkedIn)

**Length:** 150–300 words
**Structure:**
- Line 1: Pattern interrupt or tension statement (no greeting, no "Hey guys")
- Body: Hook-to-belief bridge in 3–5 short paragraphs
- Closing: Soft CTA or thought-provoking question
- No hashtags unless explicitly requested
- LinkedIn: Slightly more professional tone, first-person authority voice
- Facebook: Slightly more conversational, community-facing tone

---

### EMAIL

**Length:** 250–400 words
**Structure:**
- Subject Line: Curiosity-based or tension-based, under 50 characters
- Preview Text: Complements subject line, adds intrigue
- Opening: Daily hook story, 2–3 sentences
- Bridge: Connect celebration to avatar's situation
- Belief Content: Core message for the assigned belief stage
- CTA: Single, clear, low-friction next step
- Sign-off: Warm but not sycophantic

---

### YOUTUBE SCRIPT

**Length:** Outline + full hook section (first 90 seconds written in full)
**Structure:**
- Hook (0–30 sec): Written in full — tension-based, no generic intro
- Authority Positioning (30–60 sec): Written in full — brief, woven in naturally
- Content Outline: 3–5 section headers with 2–3 bullet points each
- Mid-Video Re-engagement: One sentence placeholder
- Final Insight: One sentence placeholder
- CTA: Written in full

> Note: Full YouTube scripts are produced by the Attention Engine™ skill.
> This skill produces the brief + hook to feed that skill.

---

### LONG-FORM AUTHORITY ARTICLE

**Length:** 600–900 words
**Structure:**
- Headline: Specific, benefit-forward, no clickbait
- Opening: Daily hook as human story (2–3 paragraphs)
- Bridge Section: Connect to avatar's core problem
- Core Content: 3 insight sections, each with a subheading
- Monthly Symbol Integration: Woven into one section naturally
- Belief Stage Conclusion: Lands the specific belief shift
- CTA: Logical next step, 2–3 sentences
