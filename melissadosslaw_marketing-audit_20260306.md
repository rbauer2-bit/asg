## Executive Summary

MelissaDossLaw.com is a three-attorney family and criminal defense firm serving Florence and Northern Kentucky since 2015. On paper, the firm has genuine assets: a Super Lawyers Rising Stars attorney (2023–2026), a 2024 Pro Bono Attorney of the Year award, a former prosecutor on the criminal defense team, and 20+ blog articles that represent more content depth than most local competitors. On the website, those assets are invisible. The site scores 34/100 across six audit dimensions — a critical grade — driven by the near-total absence of conversion infrastructure, analytics, schema markup, and positioning strategy.

The single most urgent problem: there is no functional contact form anywhere on the site. The only paths to conversion are a phone call or a manually composed email — both requiring the highest commitment from a prospect who is already anxious, distressed, and cautious. The Contact Form 7 plugin is installed but not rendering. This one issue is costing the firm a measurable volume of consultation requests every week.

The second most urgent problem: the site is operating entirely without measurement. No Google Analytics, no Tag Manager, no Meta Pixel, no session recording. Every marketing decision is being made blind. There is no way to know which pages are converting, where traffic comes from, or what topics are driving calls.

Fixing the contact form and installing GA4 are two-day tasks. Combined with five additional quick wins in this report, the firm could reasonably expect a 30–50% increase in consultation requests within 30 days — without spending a dollar on advertising. Total estimated revenue impact of full implementation: $8,000–$22,000/month in incremental new matter revenue.

## Quick Wins (This Week)

**1. Fix the Contact Form 7 deployment and add the form to the contact page and homepage #contact anchor.**
Contact Form 7 is already installed. The shortcode is not rendering. Fields: First Name, Last Name, Phone (required), Email (required), Practice Area (dropdown: Family Law, Divorce, Child Custody, Criminal Defense, DUI, Other), Brief Description (text area). Submit button: "Request My Free Consultation." This is the single highest-impact change on the site.
*Expected impact: 20–35% increase in consultation requests within two weeks.*

**2. Add a keyword-rich title tag to every page.**
The homepage currently has no title tag — Google is generating one automatically from body content. Recommended: `Northern Kentucky Family Law & Divorce Attorney | Melissa Doss Law` (56 chars). Each practice area page needs a unique, location-specific title.
*Expected impact: 15–25% lift in organic click-through rate within 60 days.*

**3. Write and install meta descriptions for all 12 indexed pages.**
No meta descriptions exist on any page. Homepage recommended: `Facing divorce, custody, or criminal charges in Northern Kentucky? Melissa Doss Law has protected families since 2015. Call (859) 322-5550.` (145 chars). Each page description should be unique and contain a CTA.
*Expected impact: Immediate improvement in SERP click-through rate across all pages.*

**4. Install Google Analytics 4 and Google Tag Manager.**
The site has zero analytics. Install GTM first, then deploy GA4 through it. Set up goals for: contact form submissions, phone number clicks, and email link clicks. This unlocks the ability to measure every subsequent optimization.
*Expected impact: Enables all future decision-making; required before any paid advertising.*

**5. Surface the Super Lawyers Rising Stars badge and Pro Bono Attorney of the Year award above the fold.**
Both credentials are currently buried in team bio paragraphs. Move them to a trust bar immediately below the hero headline. These are third-party validations that most competitors cannot match — they should be the first thing a cold visitor sees after the headline.
*Expected impact: 10–20% reduction in bounce rate; measurable increase in time-on-site.*

**6. Add alt text to every image on the site.**
Currently zero images have alt attributes. For team photos, use: `alt="[Attorney Name], Northern Kentucky [Practice Area] Attorney"`. This is an ADA compliance issue and a missed SEO signal across all 20+ blog post images.
*Expected impact: Incremental improvement in image search visibility; ADA compliance.*

## Strategic Recommendations (This Month)

**1. Rewrite the homepage headline and mission block to lead with outcomes, not credentials.**
"Family and Criminal Law Attorneys — Serving Northern Kentucky since 2015" communicates category, not value. The current mission statement ("Civility. Integrity. Compassion.") is indistinguishable from any competitor in the region. Rewrite the hero around the emotional reality of the prospect's situation and the specific outcome this firm delivers. Emily Costin's prosecutorial background — a rare asset for criminal defense — is not mentioned anywhere outside her bio.
*Deliverable: New homepage copy, routed to Vivienne Carr (CMO) and Declan Reyes (Hooks).*

**2. Build dedicated practice area landing pages with consultation CTAs.**
The /practice-areas URL returns a 404. No individual service pages are linked from the main navigation. Visitors searching "Northern Kentucky divorce attorney" or "Florence KY DUI lawyer" have no qualified destination. Build standalone pages for at minimum: Divorce, Child Custody, Collaborative Divorce, Criminal Defense/DUI — each with a practice-specific headline, process overview, FAQ section, and consultation form.
*Deliverable: 4–6 practice area pages; routes to Ingrid Holt (Landing Page Creator).*

**3. Add a LegalService + LocalBusiness schema block to the homepage.**
No structured data exists on the site. Implement JSON-LD with attorney name, practice areas, geographic service area (Florence, Boone County, Campbell County, Kenton County), phone, address, and opening hours. This creates eligibility for local knowledge panel enhancements and review stars in SERPs — features competitors may already be capturing.
*Implementation time: 2–3 hours for a developer or technical team member.*

**4. Fix the sitemap HTTP references and remove the indexed 404 page.**
The sitemap references HTTP instead of HTTPS, creating indexing inconsistency. The `/404-page-not-found/` URL is listed in the sitemap and being indexed by Google. The `/wills/` page references a practice area not currently offered. All three should be corrected in the sitemap configuration.

**5. Launch a Google Reviews capture and display strategy.**
A Google Reviews button exists on the site but the rating and count are not displayed. Embed a live Google Reviews widget with star rating and minimum 5 testimonial pulls on the homepage, above the contact form. Simultaneously, build a systematic post-case review request: at case close, send a text message with a direct Google Review link. Even 10–15 reviews will materially improve local pack performance.
*Routes to Marcus Chen (CRM) for automation of the review request sequence.*
