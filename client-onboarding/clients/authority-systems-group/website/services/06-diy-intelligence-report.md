## [SECTION 1 — WHO THIS IS FOR]

**HEADLINE:**
For business owners who want the foundation, not the execution.

**BODY:**
Most ASG clients engage at the Blueprint or Retainer level. The Intelligence Report is designed for a different profile — the business owner who:

- Has internal marketing capability: an in-house team, a trusted contractor, or their own sufficient capacity
- Has been running their marketing on general principles or gut instinct and wants a research foundation to build from
- Is considering a full engagement with ASG and wants to understand the quality and depth of the work before committing
- Wants a competitive and market intelligence baseline before making a significant strategic or hiring decision

The Intelligence Report does not include implementation direction, strategy recommendations, email sequences, or content plans. It is the intelligence layer only. What you do with it is yours.

## [SECTION 2 — WHAT'S IN IT]

**HEADLINE:**
Four sections. Every one specific to your niche and geography.

**BODY:**

---

**Section 1 — Competitive Landscape**
Your named local or regional competitors, analyzed specifically. What they're doing well. Where their marketing falls short. The positioning they've claimed and what they've left unclaimed. Competitor profiles include: online presence assessment, messaging review, review ecosystem analysis, and estimated content output.

No generic "the competitive landscape is competitive" commentary. Named firms. Specific analysis.

---

**Section 2 — Market Opportunity Assessment**
The size and characteristics of your addressable market in your geography. The specific segment most likely to hire you based on trigger events and demographic indicators. The revenue opportunity your market presents — calculated against realistic conversion rates, not optimistic projections.

---

**Section 3 — Avatar Profile**
The psychological profile of your highest-value prospect. Not demographics alone — belief barriers, trigger events, fears, and desires that drive the decision to hire.

Specifically:
- The trigger event that initiates their search
- The internal question they're trying to answer when they find you
- The fear they're managing throughout the decision process
- The self-image they're protecting in the hiring decision
- The enemy belief they're currently operating on — the misdiagnosis that prevents fast action
- The specific language they use when they describe their problem to friends

This section is the input every content piece, email, and consultation approach should be filtered through. Without it, content is guesswork.

---

**Section 4 — Positioning Gap & Authority Statement**
The gap between what your competitors are claiming and what your market actually needs. The positioning opportunity that's available in your specific market — not the category in general, your market specifically.

Your Authority Statement: a single positioning line that articulates why the right prospect should choose you over every alternative, built from the competitive gap and avatar profile.

## [SECTION 4 — THE PROCESS]

**HEADLINE:**
How the report is produced.

**BODY:**

**Step 1 — Intake Form**
A structured intake capturing your services, geography, primary competitor names, existing positioning, and client profile. Takes approximately 20 minutes to complete.

**Step 2 — Research & Production**
Daniel Frost's team conducts competitor research, market sizing, and avatar profiling. Roger reviews all sections before the report is finalized. Timeline: 5-7 business days from intake completion.

**Step 3 — Delivery**
The report is delivered as a formatted PDF. No debrief call is included by default. An optional 30-minute debrief call with Roger is available as an add-on — he walks through the key findings and answers questions about how to apply them.
