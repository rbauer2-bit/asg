# Authority Blueprint — Authority Systems Group

**Executive Summary (Roger, Director)**

Authority Systems Group launches as a legal-focused authority agency targeting family law firms across the continental U.S. Immediate objective: validate the Authority Content product through two paid pilot engagements and produce repeatable case studies that convert. Year‑1 revenue target is $360k (20 clients at $18k each).

Three big opportunities ahead:
1. **Pilot validation** – a rapid proof-of-concept that creates marketable case studies and seed data for every future pitch.
2. **Authority differentiation** – only a handful of competitors produce narrative, ethics-safe content for family law. We can own that gap and charge a premium.
3. **High LTV client base** – family law matters run $15k–$50k; a single converted firm sustains the agency for months.

Three major risks if we move too slowly:
- Failing to sign pilots in Q1, leaving us as a theory rather than a tested business.
- Building a generic content engine that cannot be sold to skeptical attorneys.
- Outspending early revenue on marketing before establishing repeatable onboarding.

Key priorities:
- **Fast Cash Sprint**: sign 2 pilots within 60 days, deliver results, capture testimonials.
- **Strategic infrastructure**: build a turnkey authority engine and a Belief-to-Buy content matrix.
- **Operational cadence**: codify pricing, intake, and production so the team can scale.

90‑day success metrics:
- Two pilots contracted, minimum $36k revenue.
- One public case study published with KPI lift data.
- KPI dashboard live and tracking 40 leads/month target.
- First hiring plan drafted and $6k/month paid budget approved.

## Belief-to-Buy Map (detailed)

Avatar: `Managing Partner Matt`

1) Enemy Belief — "Marketing wastes time and attracts low-quality inquiries."
   - Pre-frame: evidence that authority attracts higher-quality inquiries (sample case study snippets).
   - Content type: Comparative case study (before/after intake quality metrics).
   - CTA: Schedule a 20‑minute strategy briefing.
   - Metrics: lead quality score, discovery-to-proposal rate.

2) Problem Aware — "Referrals are declining but I can’t prioritize marketing."
   - Pre-frame: show time-leveraged authority wins (how a 1-2 hour interview generates ongoing pipeline).
   - Content type: Short playbook + time-cost comparison.
   - CTA: Request pilot scope and timeline.
   - Metrics: content engagement, email opens from firm partners.

3) Solution Skepticism — "Marketing firms don’t understand law/ethics."
   - Pre-frame: demonstrate legal-first process, redaction & ethics-safe publishing protocols.
   - Content type: Legal credibility dossier (methodology + anonymized sample).
   - CTA: Ask for references / past anonymized cases.
   - Metrics: reference requests, pilot sign rate.

4) Provider Distrust — "Vendors overpromise and underdeliver."
   - Pre-frame: offer pilot guarantees, transparent metrics and phased payments tied to milestones.
   - Content type: Pilot agreement template + KPI SLAs.
   - CTA: Sign pilot agreement.
   - Metrics: pilot closure time, churn risk signals.

5) Price Objection — "Cost vs. value."
   - Pre-frame: show ROI math using average case value and lifetime client value.
   - Content type: ROI calculator / payback timeline.
   - CTA: Request custom ROI for firm.
   - Metrics: proposal acceptance rate, time-to-payback.

Biggest barrier: Solution Skepticism / Provider Distrust — Q1 content must neutralize both using legal-specific proof and a low-risk pilot structure.

## Fast Cash Sprint — expanded tactics & responsibilities

Strategy 1 — Pilot Authority Content for 2 Firms
- Owner: Sales lead (Tanya) + Content lead
- Steps: identify pilot candidates → pitch with sample dossier → sign pilot → intake & produce → publish & measure
- Deliverables per pilot: 1 long-form case study, 4 LinkedIn posts, 1 client-approved downloadable briefing, one executive video (optional)
- Required client inputs: anonymized case summaries, 2-hour executive interview, logo and permissions
- Risk mitigation: phased payment tied to deliverables; option to redact case details
  
 - Revenue scenarios (per pilot):
    - Conservative: $18k (one-year retainer) — 1 pilot closed
    - Target: $36k (2 pilots closed)
    - Stretch: $72k (2 pilots + one upgraded premium package)
  
 - Assumptions: pilot conversion from outreach = 5% initially; average deal = $18k; discovery-to-close time = 14–30 days.

Strategy 2 — Paid LinkedIn Outreach to Firm Partners
- Owner: Growth lead
- Steps: craft InMail creative using pilot snippets → A/B test two audience segments (managing partners; practice managers) → run small budget ( $1k–$3k ) → measure CPL and quality
- KPIs: CPL, discovery calls booked, pilot sign rate
  
 - Revenue scenarios (90 days):
    - Conservative: 1 signed pilot from outreach (estimated revenue $18k)
    - Target: 3 signed pilots (estimated revenue $54k)
    - Stretch: 6 signed pilots (estimated revenue $108k)
  
 - Assumptions: CPL $150–$500 depending on targeting; discovery-to-pilot conversion 3–8%.

Strategy 3 — Referral Incentive Program
- Owner: Partnerships lead
- Mechanics: fixed referral fee (e.g., $1,500) or 10% discount on first month; limited-time offer for first 6 months
- KPIs: referrals received, conversion rate, CAC from referrals
  
 - Revenue scenarios (90 days):
    - Conservative: 1 referral converts ($18k)
    - Target: 3 referrals convert ($54k)
    - Stretch: 6 referrals convert ($108k)
  
 - Assumptions: referral conversion higher (10–25%); CAC materially lower than paid outreach.

## KPI Dashboard (20 metrics recommended)

To track progress we recommend maintaining a Google Sheet with separate tabs for acquisition, conversion, revenue, and content. Each KPI should include a formula column and a target column.

Acquisition & Top-of-Funnel
1. Website visits (monthly) — target 2,000 by month 6
2. Content views (articles published) — track by asset; goal 1,000 views/article
3. LinkedIn impressions — target 5,000/month
4. Leads — marketing qualified (MQL) — target 40/month
5. Leads — sales qualified (SQL) — formula: MQL × qualification rate (20%)

Engagement & Conversion
6. Discovery calls booked — track via calendar; target 10/week
7. Proposal sent — formula: discovery calls × 50%
8. Proposal conversion rate — formula: proposals ÷ closed deals; target 5%
9. Time to close (days) — average; target 21 days
10. Lead quality score (1–10) — subjective rating by Sales at qualification

Revenue & Value
11. New clients signed (monthly) — goal 3 in Q1, 5 in Q2
12. Average deal size — $18k baseline
13. MRR / ARR — derive from signed retainers
14. CAC (customer acquisition cost) — total acquisition spend ÷ new clients
15. LTV (estimated) — average deal size × average lifespan (3 years)

Content Performance
16. Case study downloads — goal 500/download
17. LinkedIn engagement rate (comments/shares) — target >2%

Operational
18. Content production throughput (articles/month) — baseline 2, target 5
19. Client satisfaction (NPS or simple 1–5 survey) — target 4+ average
20. Churn rate / retention — target <10% annual

Baselines & targets: monthly leads current 0 → target 40; conversion target 5%; monthly revenue target $30k.

Dashboard: Google Sheet with automated monthly tabs, visual funnel chart, and traffic sources breakdown. Use simple formulas and color-coded conditional formatting for whether KPIs are on track.
