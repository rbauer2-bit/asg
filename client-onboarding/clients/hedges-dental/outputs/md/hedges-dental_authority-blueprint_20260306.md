## 3A. Market Context — Louisville Implant Dentistry

Louisville, Kentucky is a metro of approximately 800,000 residents (Jefferson County and surrounding suburban counties). Jefferson County's dental market is mature, competitive, and in a state of rapid consolidation — corporate dental groups have opened multiple locations in the Louisville corridor in the past 36 months, and specialist referral networks have traditionally controlled the premium implant case flow.

The Louisville implant dental market has the following structural characteristics relevant to Dr. Hedges' positioning:

**High corporate market penetration.** ClearChoice Dental Implant Center operates at 9351 Viking Center Drive — in the same 40222 ZIP code as Hedges Dental. Corporate dental groups including Heartland Dental, Aspen Dental, and regional DSO operators have established multiple locations throughout Jefferson County and the eastern Louisville corridor. These operations market on convenience and brand recognition, not on the quality of the individual doctor-patient relationship.

**Specialist default routing.** Louisville's general dentistry ecosystem routes implant cases to oral surgeons and periodontists by default. Oral Surgery Consultants of Kentucky, Innovative Oral Surgery (4.9–5.0 star rating across locations), and similar groups have established strong referral pipelines that intercept patients before they ever reach a private-practice general dentist with implant capability. This default routing is not based on clinical superiority — it is based on awareness and habit.

**Consumer research window.** Implant patients research for 3–12 months before booking a consultation. They are not searching for the cheapest option — they are searching for the most trustworthy option. During that research window, they visit multiple websites, watch videos, read reviews, and build a mental shortlist. Almost no implant dentist in Louisville's suburban market has a meaningful content presence in that research phase. The window is wide open.

**Fee-for-service premium.** Louisville's implant market supports fee-for-service pricing at full case values of $3,500–$6,500 per single implant and $25,000–$65,000 for full-arch cases. ClearChoice's $50,000+ average creates a price ceiling reference that positions private practices favorably at $30,000–$45,000 — not as the "cheap" option, but as the local expert alternative at a rational price differential.

People sometimes ask why implant outcomes vary so much between practices.
