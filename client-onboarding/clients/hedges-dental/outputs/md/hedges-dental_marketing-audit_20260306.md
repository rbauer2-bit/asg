## Executive Summary

Hedges Dental scores 22 out of 100 on the ASG composite marketing scale. This is a critical-grade result — not because the practice is failing clinically, but because the marketing infrastructure does not reflect the caliber of the work being done or the ambition of the growth goal. Dr. Hedges has the clinical training, the right location, the personal story, and the community trust to own the implant positioning in his market. None of that is visible anywhere online.

The single biggest finding of this audit is a positioning problem masquerading as a marketing problem. Dr. Hedges is trying to attract high-ticket implant patients while every digital asset he controls tells the market he is a friendly, affordable neighborhood dentist. That is not a bad thing to be — it is simply not what an implant patient is looking for when they are six months into researching who to trust with a $20,000 decision. The market categorizes him as a general dentist who also does implants. Until that changes at the positioning level, no volume of ads or social posts will produce the case flow he's after.

The second major finding is a competitive vulnerability that is also a competitive opportunity: ClearChoice Dental Implant Center operates in the same ZIP code (40222) on Viking Center Drive. ClearChoice spends more on advertising in a week than most independent dental practices spend in a year. They dominate awareness for full-arch implants in Louisville. But they charge 25–40% above market rate, do not accept dental insurance, and carry consistent criticism for financial transparency. Every one of those weaknesses is a direct positioning opportunity for Dr. Hedges — and currently, none of them are being exploited.

The third critical finding: Dr. Hedges already has a second domain — toplouisvilledentist.com — with a dedicated All-on-4 page and implant content. This shows real strategic intent that is ahead of most general dentists attempting the implant transition. The problem is that neither site is developed to the point where an implant-researching patient would feel confident enough to book. The infrastructure exists. The authority content does not.

At full maturity, implementing the recommendations in this audit is estimated to generate an additional $14,000–$32,000 per month in implant-case revenue (see Revenue Impact Summary for methodology). The highest-impact moves — reactivating dormant implant-diagnosed patients, rebuilding toplouisvilledentist.com as an implant-authority conversion site, and deploying a Google Business Profile strategy targeting "dental implants Louisville KY" — are executable within 30 days and require no ad spend.

### Conversion Optimization Analysis
**Score: 24/100**
*Analysis: Marcus Cole, Conversion Rate Optimization Specialist*

**CTA audit:**
Both websites feature a single primary CTA: "Request an Appointment." This is the minimum viable CTA — it works for a patient who has already decided. It does nothing for the patient who is 60 days into research and not yet ready to commit to an appointment. This site converts exactly one type of visitor: the one who already decided to call. Everyone else leaves without a trace.

**Missing conversion mechanisms:**
1. **No lead magnet or email capture.** A research-phase implant patient visits the site, finds no reason to leave their contact information, and moves on. The practice has no way to stay in front of them.
2. **No financing callout.** CareCredit is accepted. This is not visible on either site. Financing information at the point of interest is a conversion mechanism — not just a payment option. "Starting at $99/month" converts browsers into callers.
3. **No free consultation offer.** "Request an Appointment" creates friction. "Schedule Your Free Implant Consultation" eliminates the cost barrier to the first contact.
4. **No social proof near CTAs.** The "Request an Appointment" button appears with nothing around it that answers the question a patient is asking at the moment they're deciding whether to click: "Can I trust this person?"
5. **No urgency or scarcity mechanism.** Even a soft urgency signal — "Dr. Hedges has limited consultation slots available each week" — increases CTA click-through.
6. **No chat or callback option.** A portion of implant-curious patients will not fill out a form but will start a chat conversation. There is no chat on either site.

**Mobile experience:**
Not tested directly. However, given the site's construction and hosting profile, mobile optimization should be verified as part of the rebuild. Over 65% of dental searches are conducted on mobile devices.

**Form friction:**
The appointment request form was not examined in detail, but any form with more than 3 fields on a first-contact page for an implant prospect will see significant drop-off. The form should capture: name, phone, and "what can we help you with?" — nothing more on the first touch.

**Highest-impact conversion fix:** Replace "Request an Appointment" with "Schedule Your Free Implant Consultation" on toplouisvilledentist.com, and add a CareCredit financing callout within the visual field of that CTA. These two changes alone are estimated to increase consultation request volume by 20–35%.

**The case acceptance gap:**
Based on publicly available information, the practice appears to be using the standard dental appointment-booking model: patient calls, front desk books, patient comes in, Dr. Hedges does a consultation, patient is presented with treatment options and cost, patient says "let me think about it," and leaves. This is the default dental case acceptance cycle — and for implants, it is broken. The "let me think about it" response, when the patient has not been pre-educated about cost, process, and outcomes, has a 60–70% non-return rate.

The growth strategy must move patient education upstream — before the consultation, not during it. When a patient books a consultation having already watched three YouTube videos from Dr. Hedges, read a patient transformation story on the website, and received an email explaining the financing options, the consultation converts at dramatically higher rates. The content and nurture infrastructure in Recommendations SR-1 and LTI-2 address this directly.
