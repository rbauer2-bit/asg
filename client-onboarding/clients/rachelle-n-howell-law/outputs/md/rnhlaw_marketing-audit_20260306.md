## Executive Summary

RNH Law — Rachelle N. Howell, Attorney at Law — is a 23-year solo practice in Louisville, Kentucky, handling criminal defense, personal injury, and family law. Rachelle has the credentials most attorneys spend careers chasing: Super Lawyers Rising Stars (2015–2017, top 2.5% of Kentucky attorneys), Avvo Client's Choice Award, 22+ years of courtroom experience including a formative stretch at the Louisville Metro Public Defender's Office. The problem is not the practice. The problem is that the digital presence is not functioning — and "not functioning" is not hyperbole.

The website scores **31 out of 100**, placing it in the critical tier. The site's SSL certificate has expired, which means every visitor using Chrome, Firefox, or Safari is greeted with a "Your connection is not private" security warning before seeing a single word of content. A `site:rnhlaw.com` search query returns zero results, indicating the site has effectively been removed from Google's index. The website that is supposed to generate client inquiries is, at this moment, invisible and untrustworthy — while competitors in the same building and same practice areas maintain functioning, HTTPS-secured, indexed websites.

Three findings demand immediate action before anything else in this report matters: (1) The SSL certificate must be renewed or replaced — this is the single blocking issue that is suppressing every other marketing channel. (2) The website must be resubmitted to Google Search Console and re-indexed once HTTPS is restored. (3) The 82 existing reviews aggregated on BirdEye must be surfaced on the website — Rachelle has a review base that most solo practitioners would envy, and none of it is working for her online. The Revenue Impact Summary at the end of this report documents the methodology and timeline for each recommendation. The short version: the SSL fix alone is the prerequisite — without it, every other investment in the site is building on a broken foundation.

**A/B Test Hypotheses:**

1. **Hypothesis:** If we add a "Results" section above the fold featuring anonymized case outcomes (e.g., "Felony charge reduced to misdemeanor — Jefferson County, 2024"), then consultation request rates will increase.
   **Metric:** Consultation form submissions per 100 homepage sessions.
   **Directional Rationale:** Visitors comparing attorneys are assessing risk and capability simultaneously. Stated case outcomes shift the evaluation from credential-checking to evidence review — a much stronger decision frame. Attorneys who show results attract higher-intent visitors who have already partially pre-sold themselves.

2. **Hypothesis:** If we change the primary CTA from a standard "Free Consultation" button to "Call Tonight — Evening Appointments Available," then phone call conversion rate will increase.
   **Metric:** Phone click-through rate on homepage (tracked via call tracking).
   **Directional Rationale:** "Free Consultation" is table stakes in legal — every competitor offers it. "Call Tonight" paired with an availability signal removes the friction of "I'll do it tomorrow" and creates an immediate action path for the visitor who is worried right now about a criminal charge or accident.

3. **Hypothesis:** If we add photos of Rachelle in a professional context (courtroom, consultation, office) alongside client testimonials, then trust metrics will improve and bounce rate will decrease.
   **Metric:** Scroll depth past the testimonials section and time on page.
   **Directional Rationale:** Solo practitioners compete on personality and trust. A photo of the specific attorney a visitor is hiring is more trust-building than any credential. It makes the relationship feel real before a call is ever made, reducing the friction of "I don't know this person" that prevents first-contact.

**Missing CRO Elements:**
- Live chat or after-hours text/chat capture for criminal defense (most criminal matters happen at night)
- "What to expect at your free consultation" walkthrough — reduces last-minute no-shows and drop-off
- Landmark/practice area urgency ("Statute of limitations for car accidents in Kentucky: 2 years — don't wait")
- Video introduction from Rachelle — particularly impactful for solo practitioners

**Dimension Notes:**

**Page Structure (3/10):** Title tag reads "Louisville Lawyer | Attorney Louisville KY" — keyword-stuffed and generic. Should target specific practice area + location combinations: "Louisville Criminal Defense Attorney | DUI & Family Law | Rachelle N. Howell." Meta descriptions unknown but likely auto-generated or absent. H1 presumably matches the generic title. No evidence of structured heading hierarchy.

**Crawlability (1/10):** This is the critical failure. The SSL certificate has expired — the site serves no valid HTTPS. Google has flagged non-HTTPS sites as problematic since 2018 and actively downgrades their rankings. More importantly, a `site:rnhlaw.com` query returns zero results — the site appears to be effectively de-indexed. If Google cannot crawl and index a site, that site does not exist for any organic search purposes. This is the most severe technical SEO finding in this audit.

**Performance (3/10):** Unable to measure page load speed (SSL blocks access). The site likely runs on older infrastructure — the presence of a Blogspot blog and the HTTP-only configuration suggest a site that hasn't been modernized. No image optimization data available. Core Web Vitals status unknown.

**Content Architecture (1/10):** A site:rnhlaw.com search returns zero indexed pages. Whether practice-specific pages exist but are inaccessible due to SSL, or simply don't exist, the result is the same: no content is available to rank for targeted legal keywords. There are no DUI pages, no car accident pages, no custody pages — the high-value search queries for this practice area have no corresponding content on the domain.

**Schema & Tracking (2/10):** No confirmed Google Analytics installation. No confirmed schema markup (LocalBusiness, LegalService, Attorney). NAP inconsistency confirmed across directories: Super Lawyers shows "Howell Law PLLC" at 125 S 6th St, Louisville KY; BBB and most directories show "Rachelle N. Howell Attorney at Law" at 600 W Main St #500. This inconsistency suppresses local pack rankings and causes Google to lose confidence in the business's physical location.

**Anika's Technical Recommendations:**

**Critical (Fix immediately):**
1. Install a valid SSL certificate — Let's Encrypt is free; most hosting providers offer one-click SSL. This is the non-negotiable first step.
2. Redirect all HTTP traffic to HTTPS with a 301 permanent redirect.
3. Submit the site to Google Search Console and request indexing after SSL is confirmed.
4. Verify and update Google Business Profile — confirm address, hours, practice areas, and set up to receive reviews.

**High Priority (Fix within 2 weeks):**
5. Standardize NAP across all directories: choose one firm name (recommend "Rachelle N. Howell, Attorney at Law") and one address (600 West Main Street, Suite 500, Louisville, KY 40202) and update Avvo, Super Lawyers, Justia, Martindale, Lawyers.com, BBB, and Yelp.
6. Add schema markup: LocalBusiness, LegalService, Attorney, and AggregateRating (once review count is verified).
7. Install Google Analytics 4 — Rachelle currently has no visibility into how many visitors the site receives, where they come from, or what they do on the site.

**Medium-Term (1–3 months):**
8. Build practice-specific landing pages: DUI, criminal defense, car accidents, divorce, custody — each targeting a primary keyword + Louisville or county-specific modifier.
9. Create a Google Business Profile posting schedule (weekly posts, Q&A responses) to build local search visibility.

### How These Estimates Were Calculated

Every figure in this table is built from the same four inputs. The math is shown so it can be scrutinized, updated, and replaced with actual data as soon as analytics are in place.

**Input 1 — Estimated monthly site traffic: 50–150 visitors/month currently.**
This is a depressed estimate — not a baseline. An SSL-expired, likely de-indexed law firm website in Louisville would receive near-zero organic search traffic. The 50–150 range accounts for direct traffic (existing clients, referrals typing the URL directly) and residual indexed directory links that still drive some visitors. A properly functioning, HTTPS-secured, indexed law firm website in this niche and geography (Louisville metro, ~650,000 population, competitive legal market) should expect 300–600+ monthly visitors at baseline with no active SEO investment. Once GA4 is installed and 60 days of data is collected, this estimate must be replaced with actual data.

**Input 2 — Visitor-to-consultation-request conversion rate: 3%.**
Industry benchmark for local legal practices is 2–5% of website visitors converting to consultation requests. The conservative end (3%) is used. Note: this represents the website conversion rate only — walk-in, phone book, and referral clients who never visit the site are excluded from this model.

**Input 3 — Blended average matter value: $4,000.**
Blended across three practice areas:
- Criminal defense: $2,500–$5,000 for misdemeanor; $7,500–$15,000 for felony. Conservative blend: $3,500 per criminal matter.
- Personal injury: Contingency fee at 33%; average car accident settlement in Louisville $15,000–$25,000; fee = $5,000–$8,000. Conservative: $5,500 per PI matter.
- Family law/divorce: $3,000–$8,000 per matter. Conservative: $3,500 per family matter.
- Blended assuming roughly equal practice area mix, excluding high-value PI settlements and complex felony trials: **$4,000 per matter**.

**Input 4 — Incremental improvement, not compounding.**
Each line item is estimated independently. If multiple changes are implemented simultaneously, their effects in practice would overlap — but the table treats each in isolation to avoid inflating the total.

**One critical caveat:** These are directional estimates, not guarantees. The SSL fix is the prerequisite — without it, increased traffic cannot reach the site and conversion figures are meaningless. Once GA4 is installed and 60 days of data is collected, every estimate in this table should be replaced with figures grounded in actual traffic, conversion, and source data from this site.
