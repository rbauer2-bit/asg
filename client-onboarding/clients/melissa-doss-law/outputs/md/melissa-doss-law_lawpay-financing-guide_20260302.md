## STEP 2 — ACTIVATE PAYMENT PLANS IN LAWPAY

LawPay's payment plan feature allows you to:
- Set a total fee amount
- Define a down payment (typically the first retainer installment)
- Set payment intervals (weekly, biweekly, monthly)
- Auto-charge a stored card on each due date

**To activate**:
1. Log into your LawPay account at lawpay.com
2. Navigate to Settings → Payment Plans (or Payment Schedules, depending on your plan tier)
3. Enable payment plans for your account
4. Set default parameters:
   - Minimum down payment: suggest 25–33% of total fee (keeps the firm protected while lowering the entry barrier)
   - Payment intervals: monthly billing recommended for family law (aligns with client cash flow)
   - Auto-charge: enabled (removes the friction of clients manually paying each month)

**If payment plans are not visible in your account**: Contact LawPay support at (866) 376-0950. Some account tiers require an upgrade to access this feature.

## STEP 3 — ALTERNATIVE / SUPPLEMENT: AFFIRM FOR LEGAL / CLIENTCREDIT

If LawPay payment plans alone don't cover the financing range you need, consider adding a third-party legal financing option:

**ClientCredit** (clientcredit.com): A legal-specific financing service. The client applies for a credit line, is approved (or not) within minutes, and you're paid in full upfront. The client repays ClientCredit over time.
- No risk to the firm — you receive full payment
- Client gets a manageable monthly payment
- Application takes 5–10 minutes

**Affirm for Legal** (legal.affirm.com): Similar model. Integrated into intake platforms. Client applies at time of engagement.

**Recommendation**: LawPay payment plans first (zero setup cost, immediate). Add ClientCredit as an option for clients who need larger financing or prefer not to give you a recurring card authorization.

## STEP 4 — UPDATE YOUR INTAKE SCRIPT

The goal is to present financing as a standard option — not a last resort, not something you offer only when clients hesitate. When it's framed as standard, clients don't feel singled out, and more of them take it.

**Recommended intake script language:**

*During the consultation closing conversation, after discussing fees:*

> "We offer flexible payment arrangements for clients who prefer to spread the cost over time. It's a straightforward setup — we agree on a payment schedule that works for your situation, you authorize the installments upfront, and we get started. A lot of our clients find it makes the process much more manageable. Would that be something that would be helpful for you?"

**What to avoid**:
- Don't wait for the client to ask — many won't ask even if they need it
- Don't frame it as "if you can't afford the full amount" — this creates hesitation and shame
- Don't present it as a concession — present it as a service option

**Training note**: This language should be reviewed with whoever handles your intake calls (attorney, paralegal, or admin). Everyone who conducts consultations should know how to offer this and feel comfortable doing so.
