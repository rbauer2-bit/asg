## ABA COMPLIANCE NOTES

**Both pages — mandatory compliance elements:**

1. **No guaranteed outcomes.** Neither page implies a specific result is likely. All performance language describes credentials and capability, not outcomes. Phrases like "changes what's possible," "directly affects case outcomes," and "produces a different outcome" describe the attorney's preparation and positioning — not a promised result. This framing is compliant; it must be maintained if these pages are edited.

2. **AAML is a fellowship, not a board certification.** Both pages use "Fellow" and "Fellowship" exclusively. Neither page uses "specialist," "board certified," or "certified matrimonial lawyer" in connection with the AAML credential. This is required per ABA Model Rule 7.4 and Kentucky Supreme Court Rules. Do not alter this language.

3. **Super Lawyers attribution.** Both pages reference Super Lawyers as a peer-nominated selection representing the top 5% of attorneys. Per Super Lawyers usage guidelines, the designation should not be described as an award conferred by an independent ranking organization without appropriate context. The language used here ("peer-nominated selection") is compliant. The Super Lawyers badge, if used in web design, must include the required disclaimer: "Super Lawyers is a rating service of outstanding lawyers from more than 70 practice areas who have attained a high degree of peer recognition and professional achievement. The selection process includes independent research, peer nominations and peer evaluations."

4. **Martindale-Hubbell AV Preeminent attribution.** Both pages describe this as "the highest possible rating for both legal ability and professional ethics." This is accurate per Martindale-Hubbell's published designation criteria. The AV Preeminent badge, if used in web design, should include standard Martindale attribution per their badge usage guidelines.

5. **No attorney-client relationship created.** Both About pages describe the attorney and his qualifications — they do not address the reader's specific legal situation and do not constitute legal advice. The CTA directs the reader to schedule a discovery call, which is appropriate. No language on either page creates or implies an attorney-client relationship. This is naturally handled by the page structure and should remain so if pages are edited.

6. **Kentucky Bar Association advertising rules.** Kentucky SCR 3.130 (Rules of Professional Conduct, Rule 7.1–7.5) govern attorney advertising. All credential claims on both pages are accurate and verifiable. The "no false or misleading statements" standard (Rule 7.1) is met. The comparative language used ("unlike many family law attorneys," "most attorneys," "rarely find") is factual and tied to verifiable differentiators — not unsubstantiated superiority claims. Review against Kentucky Bar Association Advisory Ethics Opinions on attorney advertising is recommended before pages go live, particularly regarding badge usage and credential presentation.

7. **Indiana Rules of Professional Conduct.** The Indiana page is subject to Indiana Rules of Professional Conduct, which parallel the ABA Model Rules. The same compliance standards apply. The Indiana State Bar Association's Advertising and Solicitation Committee guidelines should be reviewed before the Indiana page is published.
