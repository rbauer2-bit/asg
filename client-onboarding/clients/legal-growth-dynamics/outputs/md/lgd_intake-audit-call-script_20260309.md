## PRE-CALL PREP

Before joining:

- [ ] Review any info the prospect submitted on the booking form
- [ ] Pull up their firm website — scan intake process, contact form, response language
- [ ] Check Google reviews — volume, recency, response pattern
- [ ] Open the **Metrics Capture Sheet** (Appendix A) — have it ready to fill in real time
- [ ] Have the **Revenue Gap Calculator** (Appendix B) open — you'll run the math live on the call

**What you're looking for before the call starts:**
- How long does their website form take to respond? (Test it if time allows)
- Is there a chat widget? Does it have live hours?
- Are their reviews recent, or did they drop off? (Signals referral system health)
- Do they have a clear HNW positioning signal, or is their site written for general family law?

## CALL TIMELINE — 30 MINUTES

| Block | Time | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| Open & Frame | 0:00–3:00 | Set tone; confirm it's a working session, not a pitch |
| Practice Context | 3:00–8:00 | Understand where they are; confirm avatar fit |
| Diagnostic — Speed to Lead | 8:00–14:00 | Uncover response infrastructure and contact rates |
| Diagnostic — Intake Conversion | 14:00–21:00 | Uncover consultation framework and conversion rate |
| Diagnostic — Pipeline Nurturing | 21:00–25:00 | Uncover what happens to leads who don't convert |
| Live Revenue Math | 25:00–27:00 | Run the numbers from the Metrics Capture Sheet in real time |

## SECTION 1: OPEN & FRAME (0:00–3:00)

*Tone: Confident, direct, peer-level. Not salesy. This is a working session.*

**Opening script:**

> "Thanks for making the time. I want to be straight with you about how I run these calls, because I think it's the opposite of what you've probably experienced with agencies.
>
> I'm not going to pitch you. I'm not going to walk you through a deck. What I'm going to do is ask you some direct questions about your intake process and your numbers — and at the end of 30 minutes, I'll tell you exactly what I see.
>
> You'll leave this call knowing more about where your practice is leaking than when you walked in. Whether or not we ever work together. That's the deal.
>
> Fair?"

*Wait for confirmation. Let the silence land.*

> "Good. I read your site before this call, so I have some context. Let me ask you a few quick questions to make sure I'm oriented correctly — then we're going to get into the actual numbers."

---

## SECTION 2: PRACTICE CONTEXT (3:00–8:00)

*Purpose: Confirm they're the right avatar. Understand current situation. Set baseline for the math.*

*These are conversational — not an interrogation. Move through them at a pace that feels natural.*

**Practice Overview:**

- "How long have you been practicing, and how long have you been focusing on family law?"
- "Is it just you, or do you have associates? Walk me through your team structure real quick."
- "How many new client consultations are you doing in a typical month — ballpark?"

**HNW Focus:**

- "When you say high-net-worth cases — what does that look like for your practice? What's a typical matter value on your better cases?"
- "What percentage of your current docket would you call genuinely HNW — meaning assets, complexity, and retainer size that you'd hold up as the kind of work you want more of?"
- "What does the rest of your docket look like? Is it by design or by default?"

**Current Marketing:**

- "What are you doing right now to bring in new cases? PPC, referrals, organic — walk me through it."
- "What are you spending monthly on paid marketing, roughly?"
- "Who's running it — in-house or an agency?"

**The Frustration:**

- "What made you book this call? What's the specific thing that's not working the way it should?"

*Listen closely here. This is the first signal of which system is most broken. Note their language exactly — you'll use it in the synthesis.*

---

## SECTION 3: DIAGNOSTIC — SPEED TO LEAD (8:00–14:00)

*Purpose: Uncover the actual response infrastructure and contact rate. Most firms have never measured this.*

**The Response Process:**

- "When someone submits a form on your website right now — what happens next? Walk me through the exact sequence."
- "Who gets the notification? A paralegal, you, a shared inbox, something else?"
- "What does that first response look like — an auto-reply, a phone call, a personal email?"
- "What's your target response time? And be honest — what does it actually average out to?"

**After-Hours Reality:**

- "What happens to a prospect who reaches out at 9pm on a Tuesday? Or Saturday morning?"
- "Is there anything automated that fires, or do they just sit until someone gets in Monday?"
- "What percentage of your inquiries come in outside business hours, do you know?"

**Contact Rate:**

- "Of all the leads that come in — forms, calls, referrals — what percentage do you actually end up speaking to?"
- "Do you track that number, or is that a guess?"

*If they don't know their contact rate: "That's actually very common. Most firms don't. We're going to estimate it together in a few minutes — it matters for the math."*

**Intake Staffing:**

- "Who's the first human voice a prospect hears from — is it you, a paralegal, a dedicated intake person?"
- "Have they been trained specifically on HNW prospect conversations, or is intake handled the same way regardless of case type?"

*Note what you're hearing in the Metrics Capture Sheet: estimated contact rate, response time, after-hours coverage, intake staffing.*

**Diagnostic signal to listen for:**
- No after-hours response = automatic Speed to Lead gap
- Response time >1 hour = likely losing cases to faster competitors
- Contact rate <70% = meaningful volume of paid leads never getting a conversation
- Intake handled by a paralegal with no HNW training = Speed to Lead is probably not even the primary issue — Intake Conversion is

---

## SECTION 4: DIAGNOSTIC — INTAKE CONVERSION (14:00–21:00)

*Purpose: Uncover how consultations are run and what the actual conversion rate is. This is usually where the biggest revenue gap lives.*

**Consultation Volume and Type:**

- "How many paid consultations are you doing per month right now?"
- "Is it a paid consult or free? What does the structure look like?"
- "Are you running all the consults yourself, or does your team handle some?"

**The Consultation Framework:**

- "When you're in a consultation with a HNW prospect — is there a structured framework your team follows, or does it depend on the attorney?"
- "What's the objective of the consultation in your mind — is it primarily diagnostic, primarily evaluative, or primarily a close?"
- "Where do most consultations break down? Where do prospects go cold?"

**The Conversion Rate — The Most Important Number on This Call:**

> "Here's a number most attorneys have never actually calculated. Of every ten people who sit across from you in a consultation — how many sign a retainer? Not eventually. That day or within a few days."

- "What's your current consultation-to-retention rate, as best you know it?"
- "If you don't know the exact number, let's estimate. Of your last 10 consultations, how many became clients?"

*Record this number. Industry average for HNW family law: 35-40%. High-performing firms: 50%+. You'll use this in the revenue math.*

**Post-Consultation Follow-Up:**

- "What happens to someone who comes in for a consultation and doesn't sign that day? What's your follow-up process?"
- "Do you have an automated sequence, or does someone make manual calls?"
- "How long do you follow up before a prospect goes cold in your system?"
- "Of the consultations that don't sign immediately — what percentage eventually do? Do you track that?"

**The Consultation Experience (Pre-Frame):**

- "What do prospects receive before they come in? Anything that sets expectations, positions your firm, tells them what to bring?"
- "Is there any communication between the time they book and the time they show up, besides a calendar reminder?"

*Diagnostic signal to listen for:*
- *No pre-consult communication = missed trust-building opportunity*
- *No structured framework = conversion rate is entirely personality-dependent*
- *Single manual follow-up = probably losing 15-20% of consultable prospects who needed more time*
- *Consultation-to-retention rate below 40% = Intake Conversion is the primary system gap*

---

## SECTION 5: DIAGNOSTIC — PIPELINE NURTURING (21:00–25:00)

*Purpose: Quantify the dormant asset they're sitting on. Most attorneys have no idea how large this number is.*

**Cold Leads:**

- "Leads that come in and never schedule a consultation — what happens to them in your system? Do they stay in a CRM, a spreadsheet, or do they just fall off?"
- "Roughly how many of those do you accumulate in a typical month?"
- "Has anyone on your team ever run a reactivation campaign to those cold leads — a sequence, a call campaign, anything?"

**Post-Consultation Non-Converts:**

- "The prospects who consult but don't sign — do they stay in your system in any active way? Or do they effectively go dark after a few follow-up calls?"
- "How many of those would you estimate are in your database right now?"

**Past Clients:**

- "How many clients have you served in the last three to five years, rough estimate?"
- "How are you staying in contact with them? Email newsletter, personal outreach, anything structured?"
- "In the last 12 months, how many past client referrals have you received? Do you have a sense of whether that number is growing, flat, or declining?"

**Referral Network:**

- "Do you have active relationships with estate attorneys, CPAs, financial advisors — the professionals who work with the same client base you do?"
- "Is there a systematic approach to those relationships, or is it more organic — you know some people and they send cases when they think of it?"
- "How many active referral source relationships do you have right now?"

*Diagnostic signal to listen for:*
- *No CRM or disorganized database = pipeline is leaking at the seams*
- *No reactivation history = high-value dormant asset sitting untouched*
- *Past clients not hearing from them = referral revenue being left on the table every month*
- *Organic referral relationships only = Nurturing is the highest-leverage long-term system*

---

## SECTION 6: LIVE REVENUE MATH (25:00–27:00)

*This is the over-deliver. Run these calculations out loud, with the prospect on the call. Use the numbers they gave you — approximations are fine. The point is specificity to their practice.*

*Open the Revenue Gap Calculator (Appendix B) and talk through it:*

> "Okay. Let me show you what I'm seeing in the numbers. This is rough — we're using estimates — but this is how I want you to think about what these systems are worth to your practice."

---

### Revenue Gap 1 — Speed to Lead

> "You told me you're getting roughly [X] inquiries per month and your contact rate is around [Y]%.
>
> If your contact rate is 50% right now — and that's not unusual — you're having a conversation with half your leads. The other half are going cold without ever speaking to anyone.
>
> If we move your contact rate from 50% to 80%, that's [X × 30%] additional conversations per month.
>
> At your consultation-to-hire rate of [Z]% and an average case value of [$A], that's [X × 30% × Z% × $A] in additional monthly revenue — just from the leads you're already paying for."

*Write the number down and say it.*

---

### Revenue Gap 2 — Intake Conversion

> "Your consultation-to-retention rate is around [Z]%. The benchmark for a well-run HNW intake system is 50%.
>
> You're doing [C] consultations per month. The gap between [Z]% and 50% is [50-Z]%.
>
> That's [C × (50-Z)%] additional retained clients per month — same consultation volume, better system.
>
> At [$A] average case value, that's [C × (50-Z)% × $A] in additional monthly revenue."

*Write the number. Say it slowly.*

---

### Revenue Gap 3 — Pipeline Nurturing

> "You have roughly [D] cold leads and [E] past clients in your database who haven't referred anyone in the past year.
>
> A structured reactivation campaign on a list that already knows you typically converts at 3 to 7%. Conservatively — call it 4%.
>
> That's [(D+E) × 4%] cases from assets you already have. At [$A] average value, that's [(D+E) × 4% × $A].
>
> That's not a marketing number. That's a database number — and it's already in your possession."

*Write the number.*

---

### Total Revenue Gap

> "Add those three together. That's the range of what these three systems are worth to your practice every month if they're built correctly.
>
> I'm not telling you we can capture all of it. But if we capture half — that's [total × 50%] in monthly revenue.
>
> And none of it requires you to spend more on ads."

*Pause. Let the math settle.*

---

## SECTION 7: SYNTHESIS & RECOMMENDATION (27:00–30:00)

*Restate what you heard. Be specific. Name the priority system. Don't hedge.*

**The Synthesis Script:**

> "Here's what I heard on this call.
>
> [Restate 2-3 specific things they said — use their exact words.]
>
> Based on what you've told me, the system that's costing you the most right now is [Speed to Lead / Intake Conversion / Pipeline Nurturing — use the Prioritization Logic in Appendix C].
>
> The reason I say that is [specific evidence from the call — their response time, their conversion rate, their database situation, etc.].
>
> Before anything else — before we talk about ads, before we talk about SEO, before we change anything else — that system needs to be built. Everything else amplifies a broken foundation.
>
> What would change in your practice if [describe the fixed system in one sentence]?"

*Let them answer. That answer is your buy signal.*

---

**The Honest Close:**

> "Here's where I land.
>
> What you've described today is very fixable. The gap isn't your marketing, it isn't your skills, and it isn't your market. It's the infrastructure between the lead and the retainer.
>
> If you want to understand what building that looks like for your specific practice — what the scope is, what the sequence is, what the investment looks like — the next conversation is a strategy session where we go deeper.
>
> If you're not there yet, I understand that completely. But I want to leave you with this: the [primary gap system] we talked about today — that's costing you [$X] every month it goes unfixed. That math doesn't change.
>
> Whatever you decide to do with that, today was worth it."

*If they want to move forward:*

> "Then let's schedule a strategy session. It's typically 60 minutes — no charge. I'll do some prep work on your market before we talk, and we'll map out exactly what a system-first approach looks like for your firm.
>
> Does [specific date/time] work for you?"

## APPENDIX C — PRIORITIZATION DECISION TREE

*Use this to determine which system to lead with in the synthesis.*

```
START HERE
│
├─ Is their contact rate below 65%?
│   └─ YES → Speed to Lead is Priority 1
│           (They're losing leads before any conversation happens.
│            Fix the infrastructure first — everything else is moot.)
│
├─ Is their response time over 1 hour on average?
│   └─ YES → Speed to Lead is Priority 1
│           (HNW prospects are gone within hours. Speed is the first fix.)
│
├─ Is their consultation-to-hire rate below 40%?
│   └─ YES → Intake Conversion is Priority 1
│           (High contact rate, but cases are walking. The leak is in the room.)
│
├─ Is their post-consultation follow-up minimal or nonexistent?
│   └─ YES → Intake Conversion is Priority 1
│           (They're abandoning cases that needed one more touchpoint.)
│
├─ Do they have a sizable database (100+ contacts) with no systematic nurture?
│   └─ YES + contact rate and conversion are both acceptable → Pipeline is Priority 1
│           (Fastest cash from existing assets — reactivation before amplification.)
│
└─ ALL THREE GAPS ARE ROUGHLY EQUAL?
    └─ Default sequence: Speed to Lead → Intake Conversion → Pipeline Nurturing
       (Build in this order. Each system supports the next.)
```

**Priority Framing by System:**

| Priority System | Opening Line in Synthesis |
|---|---|
| Speed to Lead | "The cases are coming in. They're just not being captured before your competitors get to them." |
| Intake Conversion | "The leads are reaching you. The consultation is where the case is being won — and right now, it's being decided against you more often than it should be." |
| Pipeline Nurturing | "You have a database full of people who already trust you — past clients, warm leads, referral sources. They're just not hearing from you." |

## APPENDIX D — POST-CALL PROTOCOL

Immediately after the call:

- [ ] Complete Metrics Capture Sheet if any fields are blank
- [ ] Run final Revenue Gap Calculator totals and record
- [ ] Note primary system gap and supporting evidence
- [ ] Note their exact words from "The Frustration" section
- [ ] If they're moving to strategy session: prep the market brief before the 60-min call
- [ ] If they're not ready: add to 30-day follow-up sequence (do not let them go cold)
- [ ] If they're clearly not the right avatar: close graciously and do not pursue

**Qualification Gate:**
Before booking a strategy session, confirm:
- [ ] 3+ years in practice
- [ ] At least some HNW family law in current docket or explicit intent to shift
- [ ] Monthly marketing spend or referral volume sufficient to justify system investment
- [ ] Practice owner or decision-maker was on the call
- [ ] Their frustration aligns with a system gap (not a fundamentally different problem)
