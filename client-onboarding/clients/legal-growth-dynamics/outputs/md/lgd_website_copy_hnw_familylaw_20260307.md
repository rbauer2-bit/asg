## SECTION: WHAT A BROKEN INTAKE LOOKS LIKE

- A prospect submits a contact form and waits more than 30 minutes for a response during business hours
- Your after-hours inquiry process depends on voicemail or "we'll call you back tomorrow"
- Your intake team (or you) treats the first call as a fact-gathering exercise rather than a trust-building conversation
- You have no structured follow-up sequence for prospects who consult but don't sign immediately
- You cannot tell me your consultation-to-retention rate off the top of your head

A single HNW divorce case can represent $25,000 to $150,000 or more in revenue. The intake failures above are not inconveniences. They are significant, measurable financial losses.

- Every inquiry receives an intelligent, branded response within minutes — regardless of time of day
- Your first-contact conversation is structured to build authority and confidence before the consultation
- Prospects who do not schedule immediately enter a nurture sequence that keeps your firm present and credible until they are ready to move
- Your consultation process closes at a rate that reflects your actual skill and market reputation
- You can measure exactly where prospects are in the pipeline and why they did or did not convert

This is not a theoretical improvement. The difference between a 20% and a 40% consultation-to-retention rate — on the same volume of inquiries — can represent six figures or more in annual revenue for a well-positioned family law practice.
