Today is World Autism Awareness Day — a day about understanding how differently wired minds process the world.

When a potential client calls a family law attorney, they are almost never in a calm, rational decision-making state. They are under stress. They are afraid. Their threat-detection is running high and their analytical mind is running low. This is true whether their IQ is 85 or 165.

The family law firms winning the best clients aren't the most aggressive marketers. They're the ones who figured out how to feel safe to someone who is genuinely scared.

Not impressions. Not traffic. Recognition that says: *this attorney understands what I'm going through.*

Most attorneys run annual physicals.

Today is World Health Day. And since I work with law firms all day, I'm going to tell you what a healthy family law practice looks like — and the four vital signs I check when a managing partner tells me their pipeline "isn't where they want it to be."

**Vital sign 1: Pipeline source concentration.** If more than 70% of your new matters come from one referral source or one category of source, your practice isn't healthy — it's dependent. One relationship change ends your growth.
